Question and Answer

Q: Can I wear running shoes for walking?

A: While running shoes may provide support and cushioning, they are not ideal for walking. Running shoes have a thicker sole, which can lead to tripping when walking. Additionally, running shoes lack the necessary support for individuals with flat feet or overpronation.

Q: How long should walking shoes last?

A: Walking shoes should last between 300 to 500 miles, equivalent to six months to a year, depending on usage. However, factors such as walking surface, shoe quality, and walking style can affect the lifespan of your shoes.

Q: Can I wear walking shoes for other activities?

A: While walking shoes are designed for walking, they can be worn for other low-impact activities such as light hiking, yoga, and gym workouts. However, they may not provide the necessary support for high-impact activities such as running and jumping.

Q: Can I wash my walking shoes?

A: Yes, you can wash your walking shoes. However, it is recommended to check the manufacturer's instructions before washing your shoes. Some shoes may require hand washing, while others can be machine washed.
