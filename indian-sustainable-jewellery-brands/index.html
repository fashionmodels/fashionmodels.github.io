Discovering Indian Sustainable Jewellery Brands

Written by Frank Aug 16, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of buying jewellery that is not ethically sourced or environmentally friendly? Do you want to support local artisans and businesses? Look no further than Indian sustainable jewellery brands!

Many people are becoming more conscious of the impact their purchases have on the world. The jewellery industry is no exception. Traditional jewellery making methods involve the use of harmful chemicals and practices that can harm the environment and the people involved in the production process. Additionally, many companies do not provide fair wages or safe working conditions for their workers.

Indian sustainable jewellery brands aim to create beautiful, high-quality jewellery that is made using sustainable and ethical practices. From recycled materials to fair-trade gold, these brands are committed to reducing their environmental impact and supporting local communities.

In this article, we will explore some of the best Indian sustainable jewellery brands and what sets them apart.

Target of Indian Sustainable Jewellery Brands

Indian sustainable jewellery brands target individuals who want to make a positive impact with their purchases. These brands are perfect for those who value sustainability, ethical production practices, and supporting local businesses. Whether you are in the market for a statement piece or everyday wear, there is a sustainable jewellery brand for you.

Best Indian Sustainable Jewellery Brands

1. Tribe by Amrapali: This brand is committed to using recycled silver and gold in their products. They also ensure that all their stones are ethically sourced and traceable. Tribe by Amrapali offers a range of jewellery, from statement pieces to delicate everyday wear.

2. Studio Metallurgy: This brand creates unique, handcrafted jewellery using sustainable materials such as recycled brass and silver. They are committed to ethical production practices and supporting local communities.

3. Misho: Misho is known for its minimalist, sculptural designs. They use recycled silver and gold in their pieces and ensure that all their stones are ethically sourced. Misho's jewellery is perfect for those who want to make a statement without sacrificing sustainability.

4. Soko: Soko is a fair-trade jewellery brand that works with local artisans in Kenya to create unique, sustainable pieces. They use eco-friendly materials such as recycled brass and reclaimed cow bone.

Sustainable Materials Used in Indian Sustainable Jewellery Brands

Indian sustainable jewellery brands use a variety of sustainable materials in their products. Recycled silver and gold are popular choices, as they reduce the need for newly mined metals. Other brands use sustainable materials such as recycled brass and reclaimed cow bone. Additionally, many brands ensure that their stones are ethically sourced and traceable.

How to Shop for Indian Sustainable Jewellery Brands

When shopping for Indian sustainable jewellery brands, it is important to do your research. Look for brands that are transparent about their production practices and materials. Check to see if they use recycled or sustainable materials and if they ensure that their stones are ethically sourced. Additionally, consider the brand's impact on local communities and if they provide fair wages and safe working conditions for their workers.

Personal Experience with Indian Sustainable Jewellery Brands

As someone who values sustainability and ethical production practices, I have been drawn to Indian sustainable jewellery brands. I recently purchased a necklace from Tribe by Amrapali and was blown away by the quality and beauty of the piece. Knowing that it was made using recycled materials and ethically sourced stones made me feel good about my purchase.

I also love the unique designs and attention to detail that many Indian sustainable jewellery brands offer. It is refreshing to wear jewellery that is not mass-produced and has a story behind it.
