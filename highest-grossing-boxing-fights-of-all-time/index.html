The Top Grossing Boxing Fights Of All Time A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Aug 15, 2023 · 4 min read

Boxing has always been a popular sport that has captured the hearts of many people all around the world. There's something about the thrill of two fighters going head to head in the ring that has always been a draw for fans. But which fights have been the most successful in terms of box office revenue? In this post, we'll take a closer look at the highest grossing boxing fights of all time and explore what made them so successful.

Boxing fans are always interested in learning about the biggest and most successful fights in the sport's history. Whether you're a diehard fan or just someone who enjoys watching the occasional match, you're likely curious about which fights have brought in the most revenue. After all, the success of a boxing match can often be measured by how much money it makes.

So, what are the highest grossing boxing fights of all time? Let's take a look at some of the most memorable and profitable bouts in boxing history.

The Fight of the Century: Muhammad Ali vs. Joe Frazier (1971)

The fight of the century was the highly anticipated bout between two of the greatest boxers of all time, Muhammad Ali and Joe Frazier. The fight took place on March 8, 1971, and was held at Madison Square Garden in New York City. The fight was the first of three meetings between the two fighters, and it was a close and brutal match that went the distance. In the end, Frazier won by unanimous decision, but both fighters earned a record-breaking $2.5 million for their efforts.

The Money Fight: Floyd Mayweather Jr. vs. Conor McGregor (2017)

When Floyd Mayweather Jr. and Conor McGregor announced that they would be facing off in the ring, the boxing world was abuzz with excitement. The fight, which took place on August 26, 2017, was a crossover event that saw a boxer take on an MMA fighter. Despite some initial doubts about McGregor's ability to keep up with Mayweather, the fight was a massive success in terms of revenue. The two fighters earned a combined $600 million, making it one of the highest grossing boxing fights of all time.

The Battle of the Sexes: Billie Jean King vs. Bobby Riggs (1973)

While not strictly a boxing match, the Battle of the Sexes was a tennis match between Billie Jean King and Bobby Riggs that captured the attention of the entire world. The match, which was held on September 20, 1973, was a showdown between a male and a female player at the top of their game. The match was watched by an estimated 90 million people and earned over $100 million in revenue, making it one of the most successful sporting events of all time.

The Thrilla in Manila: Muhammad Ali vs. Joe Frazier (1975)

The Thrilla in Manila was the third and final meeting between Muhammad Ali and Joe Frazier. The fight, which took place on October 1, 1975, was a brutal and exhausting match that pushed both fighters to their limits. In the end, Ali emerged as the winner, but both fighters earned a then-record purse of $5 million each. The fight is considered one of the greatest in boxing history and is remembered as a true test of endurance and skill.

Conclusion of Highest Grossing Boxing Fights of All Time

Boxing has produced some of the most exciting and memorable sporting events of all time, and the highest grossing boxing fights of all time are a testament to the sport's enduring popularity. Whether it's the intense rivalry between two fighters or the spectacle of a crossover event, these fights have captured the attention of millions of fans and generated huge amounts of revenue. So, the next time you're watching a boxing match, remember that you're witnessing a sport with a rich history and an impressive legacy.
