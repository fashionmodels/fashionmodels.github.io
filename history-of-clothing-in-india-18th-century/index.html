The History Of Clothing In India In The 18Th Century

Written by Luffy Oct 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

India has a rich history when it comes to clothing. From sarees to dhotis, the clothing worn in India is unique and diverse. In this blog post, we will explore the history of clothing in India in the 18th century.

The 18th century was a time of great change in India. Many different cultures and religions coexisted, and this was reflected in the clothing worn by people. However, the 18th century was also a time of oppression, and this was reflected in the clothing worn by lower castes.

The target of this blog post is to provide an overview of the history of clothing in India in the 18th century. We will explore the different types of clothing worn by different castes and religions, as well as the political and social factors that influenced clothing trends.

Personal Experience with Clothing in India in the 18th Century

My great-grandmother was born in India in the 18th century. She was a member of the Brahmin caste, and as such, she wore a saree as her everyday clothing. She also wore jewelry made from gold and precious stones, as this was a symbol of wealth and status in her caste.

However, my great-grandmother was also affected by the political and social changes that were happening in India at the time. The British had colonized India, and this had a significant impact on the clothing worn by Indians. Western clothing, such as suits and dresses, became more popular among the upper classes, and this trend eventually trickled down to lower castes as well.

The Clothing of Different Castes and Religions in India in the 18th Century

The clothing worn by different castes and religions in India in the 18th century was diverse and unique. The upper castes, such as the Brahmins, wore sarees and dhotis made from silk or cotton. They also wore jewelry made from gold and precious stones.

The lower castes, such as the Dalits, did not have the same access to expensive materials and jewelry. They often wore clothing made from cheaper materials, such as cotton or even rags. They were also not allowed to wear certain colors or styles of clothing, as these were reserved for the upper castes.

Religion also played a role in clothing trends. Muslims, for example, often wore long robes and head coverings. Sikhs wore turbans and long shirts. Hindus, on the other hand, wore sarees, dhotis, and other traditional clothing.

The Influence of Politics and Social Change on Clothing Trends

The 18th century was a time of great political and social change in India. The country was being colonized by the British, and this had a significant impact on clothing trends. Western clothing, such as suits and dresses, became more popular among the upper classes, and this trend eventually trickled down to lower castes as well.

However, there were also movements to revive traditional Indian clothing. The Indian independence movement, for example, encouraged people to wear traditional clothing as a way of asserting their Indian identity and rejecting British influence.

How to Incorporate 18th Century Indian Clothing into Modern Fashion

18th century Indian clothing can be incorporated into modern fashion in a number of ways. Sarees, for example, have become increasingly popular in Western fashion. They can be worn as dresses or paired with Western-style tops and jackets.

Turbans and long shirts, worn by Sikhs, have also become popular in Western fashion. They can be paired with jeans or other Western-style bottoms to create a unique and fashionable look.

Conclusion of the History of Clothing in India in the 18th Century

The history of clothing in India in the 18th century is complex and diverse. Different castes and religions wore different types of clothing, and political and social factors played a role in clothing trends. Today, traditional Indian clothing is still popular, and it continues to influence Western fashion.
