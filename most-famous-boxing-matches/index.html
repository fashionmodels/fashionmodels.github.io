The Rumble in the Jungle

The Rumble in the Jungle was a heavyweight championship fight between Muhammad Ali and George Foreman that took place in Kinshasa, Zaire (now the Democratic Republic of Congo) in 1974. This fight is widely regarded as one of the greatest upsets in sports history. Foreman was the reigning heavyweight champion and was considered unbeatable, while Ali was seen as past his prime.

However, Ali used a strategy that he called the "rope-a-dope" to tire Foreman out and then knocked him out in the eighth round. This fight is significant because it marked Ali's return to the top of the boxing world and cemented his status as one of the greatest boxers of all time.

The Thrilla in Manila

The Thrilla in Manila was the third and final fight between Muhammad Ali and Joe Frazier. This fight took place in Manila, Philippines, in 1975 and is widely regarded as one of the greatest boxing matches of all time. The fight was incredibly brutal, with both fighters taking a tremendous amount of punishment.

However, Ali emerged victorious after Frazier's trainer threw in the towel before the start of the 15th round. This fight is significant because it marked the end of the Ali-Frazier rivalry and is still talked about today as one of the greatest fights in boxing history.

The Fight of the Century

The Fight of the Century was a heavyweight championship fight between Muhammad Ali and Joe Frazier that took place in 1971. This fight was significant because it was the first time that two undefeated heavyweight champions had fought each other. The fight was incredibly close, with Frazier emerging victorious in a unanimous decision.

However, this fight was also significant because it marked Ali's return to boxing after being banned for three years for refusing to be drafted into the military. This fight was seen as a symbol of the culture wars that were taking place in the United States at the time.

The Battle of the Sexes

The Battle of the Sexes was a boxing match between Billie Jean King and Bobby Riggs that took place in 1973. This fight was significant because it was a publicity stunt that was designed to prove that men were superior to women in sports. However, King emerged victorious, and the fight is now seen as a symbol of the fight for gender equality.
