Tilet

One of the most popular traditional Ethiopian clothes is the Tilet. It is a woven cotton or silk dress that is typically worn by women. The Tilet is known for its vibrant colors and intricate patterns, which are handwoven by skilled artisans. The dress is usually ankle-length, with a fitted waist and a flared skirt. The Tilet is often worn for special occasions, such as weddings and religious ceremonies.

The Habesha Kemis is another popular traditional Ethiopian garment. It is a long, flowing dress made from cotton or silk. The dress is typically decorated with colorful embroidery and patterns, and it is worn by both men and women. The Habesha Kemis is often worn for special occasions such as weddings and religious ceremonies, but it is also worn as everyday attire.

Gabi

The Gabi is a rectangular piece of cloth made from cotton or wool. It is worn as a shawl or a wrap by both men and women, and it is often used as a symbol of hospitality. The Gabi is typically white or cream-colored, although it can be found in other colors as well.
