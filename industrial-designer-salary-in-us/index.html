How Much Do Industrial Designers Make In The Us

Written by Petter Aug 28, 2023 · 4 min read

As an industrial designer, you may be curious about how much you can expect to make in the United States. It can be frustrating to not know what salary to expect when you're first starting out or if you're considering a career change. However, there are ways to get a good idea of what you can expect.

If you're considering becoming an industrial designer in the US, you may have some concerns about the potential salary. After all, you want to be able to support yourself and your family. You may also have some questions about how to negotiate a good salary or what factors can impact your earnings.

The average industrial designer salary in the US is around $70,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on a number of factors, including your level of experience, the industry you work in, and the location of your job.

To get a better idea of what you can expect to earn as an industrial designer, it's important to consider some of these factors in more detail.

Experience Level

Your level of experience can have a big impact on your salary as an industrial designer. If you're just starting out, you can expect to earn less than someone who has been working in the field for several years. However, with time and experience, you can expect your salary to increase.

Industry

Another factor that can impact your salary as an industrial designer is the industry you work in. Some industries may pay more than others, depending on the demand for designers in that field. For example, the aerospace industry may pay more than the toy industry.

Location

Finally, your location can also impact your salary as an industrial designer. Salaries can vary depending on where you live and work. For example, industrial designers in New York City may earn more than those in a smaller city with a lower cost of living.

Negotiating Your Salary

When negotiating your salary as an industrial designer, it's important to do your research and come prepared. Consider your level of experience, the industry you work in, and the location of your job. Look at job postings and industry reports to get a good idea of what the going rate is for someone in your position.

Personal Experience

When I first started out as an industrial designer, I was earning around $50,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and moved up in my company, my salary increased to around $85,000 per year. I found that negotiating my salary was important, and I did my research to make sure I was getting paid what I was worth.

Factors That Can Impact Your Salary

As I mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can impact your salary as an industrial designer. These include your level of experience, the industry you work in, and the location of your job. However, there are also other factors to consider, such as your education level, the size of the company you work for, and the specific job duties you have.

Educational Level

Industrial designers with a higher level of education, such as a master's degree, may be able to earn more than those with only a bachelor's degree. Additionally, ongoing education and training can help you stay competitive in the job market and increase your earning potential.

Q&A

Q: What is the average industrial designer salary in the US?

A: The average industrial designer salary in the US is around $70,000 per year.

Q: What factors can impact my salary as an industrial designer?

A: Your level of experience, the industry you work in, and the location of your job can all impact your salary as an industrial designer.

Q: How can I negotiate my salary as an industrial designer?

A: Research the going rate for someone in your position, and come prepared to negotiate based on your level of experience, the industry you work in, and the location of your job.

Q: How can I increase my earning potential as an industrial designer?

A: Consider furthering your education, staying up-to-date with industry trends and technology, and seeking out job opportunities in high-paying industries.

Conclusion

As an industrial designer in the US, you can expect to earn a salary of around $70,000 per year on average. However, your actual salary will depend on a number of factors, including your level of experience, the industry you work in, and the location of your job. By doing your research and negotiating your salary, you can ensure that you're getting paid what you're worth.
