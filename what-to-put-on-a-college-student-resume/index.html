What To Put On A College Student Resume A Simple Guide

Written by Christine Aug 13, 2023 · 5 min read

As a college student, it can be challenging to know what to put on your resume. You might have limited work experience, or you might not know how to showcase your skills to potential employers. However, having a strong resume is essential for landing a job or internship. In this post, we'll discuss the essential elements of a college student resume and provide some tips on how to make yours stand out.

When it comes to what to put on a college student resume, the biggest pain point is usually the lack of work experience. Many students feel like they don't have much to offer since they haven't had many jobs, but that's not true. There are still plenty of ways to showcase your skills, even if you haven't had a traditional job.

The most critical elements of a college student resume include:

1. Contact Information

Make sure to include your name, phone number, email address, and LinkedIn profile (if you have one) at the top of your resume. This information should be easy to read and clearly visible.

2. Education

List your college or university, your major, your expected graduation date, and your GPA (if it's above a 3.0). You can also include relevant coursework or academic awards.

3. Relevant Experience

Even if you haven't had a traditional job, you can still include relevant experience on your resume. This can include internships, volunteer work, leadership positions in clubs or organizations, or freelance work. Make sure to highlight the skills you gained in these experiences and how they relate to the job you're applying for.

4. Skills

List any relevant skills you have, such as computer skills, language proficiency, or specific software knowledge. Make sure to tailor this section to the job you're applying for.

In summary, a college student resume should include your contact information, education, relevant experience, and skills. Now, let's dive deeper into each section.

Contact Information

Your contact information should be easy to find and read. Make sure to include your full name, phone number, and email address at the top of your resume. You can also include your LinkedIn profile, but only if it's up-to-date and professional.

For your phone number and email address, use a professional-sounding email address (not something like "partygirl@gmail.com") and a phone number where you can be easily reached. If you have a personal website or online portfolio, you can include that as well.

Education

Your education section should include your college or university, your major, your expected graduation date, and your GPA (if it's above a 3.0). If you've taken any relevant coursework or received academic awards, you can include that as well.

If you have a lot of relevant coursework, you can create a separate section for it. For example, if you're applying for a marketing internship and you've taken several marketing classes, you can list those classes under a "Relevant Coursework" section.

Relevant Experience

Even if you haven't had a traditional job, you still have experience that can be relevant to potential employers. Internships, volunteer work, leadership positions in clubs or organizations, and freelance work can all be included on your resume.

When listing your experience, make sure to highlight the skills you gained in each role and how they relate to the job you're applying for. For example, if you were the treasurer of your school's finance club, you can highlight your budgeting and financial management skills.

Skills

Your skills section should include any relevant skills you have, such as computer skills, language proficiency, or specific software knowledge. Make sure to tailor this section to the job you're applying for. For example, if you're applying for a marketing position, you can highlight your social media skills.

Now that you know what to put on a college student resume, it's time to start crafting yours. Remember to tailor your resume to each job you apply for and highlight your relevant skills and experience. Good luck!

Personal Experience

When I was applying for internships during my junior year of college, I felt overwhelmed by the process. I didn't have much work experience, and I wasn't sure how to showcase my skills. However, I found that highlighting my leadership positions in clubs and organizations, as well as my relevant coursework, helped me stand out. I also made sure to tailor my resume to each job I applied for, which made a big difference. I ended up landing a great internship that helped me gain valuable experience and skills.

Common Questions About What To Put On A College Student Resume

1. What if I don't have any work experience?

Even if you haven't had a traditional job, you can still include relevant experience on your resume. This can include internships, volunteer work, leadership positions in clubs or organizations, or freelance work. Make sure to highlight the skills you gained in these experiences and how they relate to the job you're applying for.

2. Should I include my high school education on my resume?

No, you should only include your college or university education on your resume.

3. How long should my resume be?

Your resume should be one page long, especially if you're a college student with limited work experience.

4. Can I include references on my resume?

No, you should not include references on your resume. Instead, have a separate list of references that you can provide to potential employers if they ask for it.

Conclusion of What To Put On A College Student Resume

Creating a strong college student resume is essential for landing a job or internship. Make sure to include your contact information, education, relevant experience, and skills. Tailor your resume to each job you apply for and highlight your relevant skills and experience. Good luck!
