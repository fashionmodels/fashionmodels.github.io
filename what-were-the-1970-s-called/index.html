What Were The 1970 S Called

Written by Robby Jul 08, 2023 · 5 min read

The 1970s was a decade of change, from the music to the fashion, and everything in between. Many people often wonder what the 1970s were called. Was it the "Disco Era", the "Me Decade", or something else entirely? In this article, we will explore the various names that were given to the 1970s, and what they meant for those who lived through this iconic decade.

The 1970s were a time of political turmoil, social change, and economic instability. Many people felt disillusioned with the government and society as a whole. The Vietnam War, Watergate scandal, and rising inflation rates were just some of the issues that plagued the United States during this time. People were looking for a way to escape the problems of their daily lives, and the entertainment industry provided just that.

The 1970s were called many things, including the "Disco Era", the "Me Decade", and the "Age of Aquarius". Each of these names represented a different aspect of the 1970s. The Disco Era, for example, referred to the rise of disco music and dance clubs. The Me Decade was a term coined by writer Tom Wolfe to describe the self-absorbed nature of the time, as people focused on personal growth and self-improvement. The Age of Aquarius was a term used to describe the spiritual and cultural awakening that many people experienced during this time.

In summary, the 1970s were a decade of change and transformation. Many different names were given to this time, each representing a different aspect of the era. Whether you remember it as the "Disco Era", the "Me Decade", or the "Age of Aquarius", the 1970s will always be remembered as a time of cultural significance.

The Disco Era

I remember the Disco Era like it was yesterday. I used to go to the disco clubs with my friends and dance the night away. The music was infectious, and the atmosphere was electric. Disco represented a break from the problems of the world, a chance to let loose and have fun. It was a time of glitter, platform shoes, and bell-bottoms. The disco era represented a new form of music, dance, and style that had a lasting impact on the world.

The Me Decade

The Me Decade was a time of introspection and self-discovery. People were focused on personal growth and self-improvement. It was a time of therapy sessions, self-help books, and meditation. The term "Me Decade" was coined by Tom Wolfe in a 1976 article for New York magazine. He wrote, "The new alchemical dream is: changing one's personality...remaking, remodeling, elevating, and polishing one's very self...and observing, studying, and doting on it." The Me Decade was a time of self-absorption, but it also represented a desire for personal transformation and growth.

The Age of Aquarius

The Age of Aquarius was a term used to describe the spiritual and cultural awakening that many people experienced during the 1970s. It was a time of peace, love, and harmony. People were interested in Eastern philosophy, meditation, and alternative forms of spirituality. The term "Age of Aquarius" was popularized by the musical "Hair", which premiered in 1967. The song "Aquarius" included the lyrics, "This is the dawning of the Age of Aquarius..." The Age of Aquarius represented a new era of consciousness and a rejection of the materialistic values of the past.

The Legacy of the 1970s

The 1970s were a time of change and transformation. The legacy of this era can still be seen today in music, fashion, and politics. Disco music has influenced modern dance music, and the fashion of the 1970s has made a comeback in recent years. The Me Decade represents a desire for personal growth and self-improvement that is still relevant today. The Age of Aquarius represents a rejection of materialistic values that is still present in alternative forms of spirituality. The 1970s were a time of cultural significance that will forever be remembered.

Question and Answer

A: The Me Decade was a term coined by writer Tom Wolfe to describe the self-absorbed nature of the time, as people focused on personal growth and self-improvement.

Q: What was the Disco Era?
A: The Disco Era referred to the rise of disco music and dance clubs. It represented a break from the problems of the world, a chance to let loose and have fun.

Q: What was the Age of Aquarius?
A: The Age of Aquarius was a term used to describe the spiritual and cultural awakening that many people experienced during the 1970s. It represented a rejection of materialistic values and a desire for a new era of consciousness.

Conclusion of What Were the 1970's Called

The 1970s were a time of change and transformation. They were called many things, including the "Disco Era", the "Me Decade", and the "Age of Aquarius". Each of these names represented a different aspect of the era, from the rise of disco music to the desire for personal growth and self-improvement. The legacy of the 1970s can still be seen today in music, fashion, and politics, making it a decade of cultural significance that will always be remembered.
