Best Women s Non Slip Shoes For Plantar Fasciitis

Written by William Oct 29, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of dealing with the constant pain and discomfort of plantar fasciitis? Do you work in an environment where slip-resistant shoes are required? If you answered yes to either of these questions, then you are in luck! In this article, we will be discussing the best women's non slip shoes for plantar fasciitis.

Dealing with plantar fasciitis can be a real pain, both literally and figuratively. This common condition can cause stabbing pain in the heel, making it difficult to stand or walk for extended periods of time. Finding a good pair of shoes that provide adequate support and cushioning can be a game changer for those dealing with plantar fasciitis.

When it comes to finding the best women's non slip shoes for plantar fasciitis, there are a few key factors to keep in mind. First and foremost, you want to look for shoes that offer good arch support. This will help to distribute your weight more evenly across your foot and reduce the stress on your plantar fascia. You also want to look for shoes that provide plenty of cushioning, especially in the heel area. This will help to absorb shock and reduce the impact on your feet with each step.

Top Picks for Best Women's Non Slip Shoes for Plantar Fasciitis

As someone who has dealt with plantar fasciitis myself, I know just how important it is to find the right shoes. After trying out several different pairs, here are my top picks for the best women's non slip shoes for plantar fasciitis:

Birkenstock Boston Clogs

While they may not be the most stylish option out there, Birkenstock Boston Clogs are a great choice for anyone dealing with plantar fasciitis. These shoes offer excellent arch support and a deep heel cup that helps to stabilize your foot and reduce pronation. The cork footbed also provides plenty of cushioning, making these shoes a great choice for anyone who spends a lot of time on their feet.

New Balance 626v2 Work Shoe

If you're looking for a more traditional work shoe, the New Balance 626v2 is a great option. These shoes feature slip-resistant soles, making them perfect for anyone working in a restaurant or healthcare environment. They also offer plenty of cushioning and support, with a removable insole that can be replaced with custom orthotics if needed.

Skechers Work Sure Track

For those who prefer a more athletic look, the Skechers Work Sure Track is a great choice. These shoes feature a slip-resistant outsole and a memory foam insole that molds to the shape of your foot for maximum comfort. They also offer plenty of arch support and cushioning, making them a great choice for anyone dealing with plantar fasciitis.

How to Choose the Right Shoes for Plantar Fasciitis

When it comes to choosing the right shoes for plantar fasciitis, there are a few key things to keep in mind. First and foremost, you want to look for shoes that offer good arch support. This will help to distribute your weight more evenly across your foot and reduce the stress on your plantar fascia. You also want to look for shoes that provide plenty of cushioning, especially in the heel area. This will help to absorb shock and reduce the impact on your feet with each step.

It's also important to consider the type of activities you will be doing while wearing these shoes. If you work in a restaurant or healthcare environment, slip-resistant shoes are a must. If you plan on doing a lot of walking or running, you may want to look for shoes with a more breathable upper to prevent your feet from overheating.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can non slip shoes also provide good arch support?

A: Yes! There are several non slip shoes on the market that also offer excellent arch support. Look for shoes with a deep heel cup and a contoured footbed for maximum support.

Q: Are Birkenstocks good for plantar fasciitis?

A: Yes! Birkenstocks are a great choice for anyone dealing with plantar fasciitis. They offer excellent arch support and a deep heel cup that helps to stabilize your foot and reduce pronation.

Q: Can I wear custom orthotics with slip-resistant shoes?

A: Yes! Many slip-resistant shoes come with removable insoles that can be replaced with custom orthotics if needed.

Q: Are slip-on shoes bad for plantar fasciitis?

A: Not necessarily. While slip-on shoes may not offer as much support as lace-up shoes, there are still plenty of slip-on options out there that provide excellent arch support and cushioning.

Conclusion

Dealing with plantar fasciitis can be a real pain, but finding the right shoes can make all the difference. When shopping for the best women's non slip shoes for plantar fasciitis, be sure to look for shoes that offer good arch support and plenty of cushioning. Whether you go with a classic clog or a more athletic option, there are plenty of great shoes out there that can help to alleviate your pain and discomfort.
