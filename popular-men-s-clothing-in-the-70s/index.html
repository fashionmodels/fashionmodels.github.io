Popular Men s Clothing In The 70S A Complete Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you ever wonder how men used to dress back in the 70s? From bell-bottom jeans to platform shoes, the 70s saw a lot of unique and trendy clothing styles for men. In this article, we'll take a deep dive into the popular men's clothing in the 70s and explore the fashion trends and styles that defined the decade.

The 70s was a decade of change and experimentation, especially when it came to fashion. Men's clothing during this era was all about making a statement and standing out from the crowd. However, finding the right clothes could be a challenge, as the styles and trends were constantly evolving.

So, what was the target of popular men's clothing in the 70s? The answer is simple - it was all about being bold, confident, and unique. Men's fashion in the 70s was all about expressing oneself and showcasing individuality through clothing.

In this post, we'll explore the popular men's clothing in the 70s and related keywords such as bell-bottom jeans, platform shoes, disco shirts, and more. We'll discuss the target of each clothing item, its history, and its impact on the fashion industry.

Bell-Bottom Jeans

One of the most iconic fashion trends of the 70s was bell-bottom jeans. These flared jeans were a fashion staple for both men and women, and were often paired with platform shoes or boots. The target of bell-bottom jeans was to create a statement look that was comfortable and stylish. These jeans were perfect for dancing at disco clubs, and became a symbol of the disco era.

Personally, I remember my dad wearing bell-bottom jeans with a printed shirt and platform shoes to a family party in the 70s. He looked so cool and confident, and I was amazed at how his outfit made him stand out from the crowd.

Platform Shoes

Another popular men's clothing item in the 70s was platform shoes. These shoes had thick soles that added height to the wearer, and were often worn in bright colors or bold patterns. The target of platform shoes was to create a statement look that was both fashionable and comfortable. These shoes were perfect for dancing at disco clubs or attending concerts, and were a must-have item for any fashion-forward man.

I remember my uncle wearing platform shoes to a family wedding in the 70s. He paired them with a colorful suit and a paisley print shirt, and looked like he was ready to hit the dance floor. His outfit definitely made a statement, and I could tell that he was feeling confident and stylish.

Disco Shirts

Disco shirts were another popular men's clothing item in the 70s. These shirts were often made of shiny, silky fabrics and featured bold patterns and bright colors. The target of disco shirts was to create a flashy and eye-catching look that was perfect for dancing the night away at disco clubs.

My dad had a collection of disco shirts that he would wear to parties and events. I remember him wearing a bright orange disco shirt with bell-bottom jeans and platform shoes to a wedding, and he looked like he was ready to hit the dance floor. His shirt definitely made a statement, and I could tell that he was feeling confident and stylish.

How to Style Popular Men's Clothing in the 70s

If you want to incorporate popular men's clothing in the 70s into your wardrobe, here are some tips on how to style these items:

  • Pair bell-bottom jeans with a printed shirt and platform shoes for a bold and stylish look.
  • Wear platform shoes with a colorful suit or patterned shirt to add height and make a statement.
  • Choose a shiny, silky disco shirt in a bold pattern or bright color for a flashy and eye-catching look.

Conclusion of Popular Men's Clothing in the 70s

The 70s was a decade of change and experimentation when it came to men's fashion. From bell-bottom jeans to platform shoes to disco shirts, the popular men's clothing in the 70s was all about making a statement and showcasing individuality through clothing. By incorporating these iconic fashion trends into your wardrobe, you can create a bold and stylish look that is sure to turn heads.
