Where To Sell Vintage Clothing In Nyc

Written by Aprilia Aug 27, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Do you have a closet full of vintage clothing that you never wear? Are you looking to make some extra cash? Selling vintage clothing can be a great way to declutter your space and make some money. However, finding the right place to sell vintage clothing in NYC can be a challenge. In this article, we will explore some of the best options for selling vintage clothing in NYC.

Selling vintage clothing can be a daunting task. You might be worried about finding the right buyer or getting the best price for your items. You might also be unsure of where to start or what options are available to you. Fortunately, there are a variety of options for selling vintage clothing in NYC, each with its own pros and cons.

The first option for selling vintage clothing in NYC is to sell online. There are a variety of online marketplaces that specialize in vintage clothing, such as Etsy, Depop, and Poshmark. These platforms allow you to reach a wider audience and sell your items from the comfort of your own home. However, you will need to take good quality pictures of your items and write detailed descriptions in order to attract buyers.

If you prefer to sell your vintage clothing in person, there are several options available to you. One option is to sell at a vintage clothing market, such as the Brooklyn Flea or the Manhattan Vintage Clothing Show. These markets attract a large number of buyers and give you the opportunity to showcase your items in person. However, you will need to pay a fee to rent a booth and set up your display.

Selling to Vintage Clothing Stores

Another option for selling vintage clothing in NYC is to sell to a vintage clothing store. There are a variety of vintage clothing stores in NYC that buy vintage clothing, such as Buffalo Exchange, Beacon's Closet, and L Train Vintage. Selling to a store is a great option if you have a large quantity of items to sell, as they will often buy items in bulk. However, you may not get the highest price for your items, as the store will need to make a profit when reselling them.

Selling at Consignment Shops

Consignment shops are another option for selling vintage clothing in NYC. Consignment shops allow you to sell your items on consignment, meaning that the shop will sell your items for you and take a percentage of the sale price as commission. This is a good option if you have high-end or designer vintage clothing, as consignment shops often specialize in these types of items. However, you may need to wait a while for your items to sell, and you will need to split the profits with the consignment shop.

Conclusion of Where to Sell Vintage Clothing NYC

Selling vintage clothing in NYC can be a great way to make some extra cash and declutter your space. There are a variety of options available to you, including selling online, selling at vintage clothing markets, selling to vintage clothing stores, and selling at consignment shops. Each option has its own pros and cons, so it's important to do your research and choose the option that works best for you.

Personal Experience Selling Vintage Clothing in NYC

I have sold vintage clothing at both the Brooklyn Flea and Buffalo Exchange in NYC. While the Brooklyn Flea gave me the opportunity to showcase my items in person and connect with buyers, I found that Buffalo Exchange offered a more streamlined process for selling my items. I was able to bring in a large quantity of items and receive payment on the spot. However, I did not receive the highest price for my items, as the store needed to make a profit when reselling them.

Tips for Selling Vintage Clothing in NYC

If you're considering selling vintage clothing in NYC, here are some tips to keep in mind:

1. Do your research

Before selling your items, research the different options available to you and choose the one that works best for you. Consider factors such as fees, commission rates, and the type of items each option accepts.

2. Take good quality pictures

When selling online, take good quality pictures of your items and write detailed descriptions to attract buyers.

3. Price your items realistically

When pricing your items, be realistic about their value and condition. Check online marketplaces and vintage clothing stores to get an idea of what similar items are selling for.

4. Keep your items in good condition

Make sure your items are clean and in good condition before selling them. Consider having them professionally cleaned or repaired if necessary.

Question and Answer Section

Q: How do I know if my vintage clothing is valuable?

A: Vintage clothing can be valuable if it is rare, in good condition, and from a desirable era. Researching similar items online and consulting with vintage clothing experts can help you determine the value of your items.

Q: What should I do if I can't sell my vintage clothing?

A: If you are having trouble selling your vintage clothing, consider donating it to a thrift store or charity. You can also repurpose the fabric or use it for DIY projects.

Q: How do I ship vintage clothing to buyers?

A: When shipping vintage clothing to buyers, make sure to use a sturdy box or envelope and pack the items carefully to prevent damage. Consider using a shipping service that provides tracking and insurance.

Q: Can I sell vintage clothing that I made myself?

A: Yes, you can sell vintage clothing that you made yourself. However, it may be more difficult to determine the value of these items, as they are not from a specific era or designer.
