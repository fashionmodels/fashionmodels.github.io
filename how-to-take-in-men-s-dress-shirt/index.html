How To Take In Men s Dress Shirt

Written by Petter Jul 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your dress shirts fitting too loosely? Or maybe you lost some weight and your favorite shirt no longer fits. Altering a dress shirt can be intimidating, but with the right tools and a little bit of know-how, it can be a simple and cost-effective solution. In this tutorial, we will guide you through the process of taking in a men's dress shirt.

It can be frustrating when you find the perfect dress shirt, but it doesn't fit properly. Maybe it's too baggy around the waist or too loose in the sleeves. Whatever the issue may be, an ill-fitting dress shirt can make you feel uncomfortable and self-conscious. Altering a dress shirt can solve these issues and make you feel confident and comfortable in your clothing.

The first step in taking in a men's dress shirt is to determine how much you need to alter it. This can be done by trying on the shirt and pinching the excess fabric where it is too loose. Mark the fabric with a fabric pen or chalk where you want to take it in. It's important to take in equal amounts on each side of the shirt to ensure it remains balanced.

Once you have marked the excess fabric, remove the shirt and turn it inside out. Use a seam ripper to remove the stitches along the side seams of the shirt, starting at the bottom hem and working your way up to the armhole. Be sure to only remove the stitches that are necessary for the alteration.

Choosing the Right Stitch

When altering a dress shirt, it's important to choose the right stitch to ensure durability and a professional finish. The two most common stitches used are a straight stitch and a zigzag stitch. A straight stitch is best for lightweight fabrics, while a zigzag stitch is better for heavier fabrics or when you need more stretch in the seams.

Personally, I prefer to use a straight stitch when altering dress shirts. It provides a clean finish and is less bulky than a zigzag stitch. However, if you are altering a shirt made of a heavier fabric or one that requires more stretch, a zigzag stitch may be the better option.

Getting the Right Fit

After you have removed the necessary stitches, it's time to sew the side seams back together. Start at the bottom hem and sew up to the armhole, using the markings you made as a guide. Be sure to backstitch at the beginning and end of the seam to reinforce it.

Once you have sewn both sides of the shirt, try it on to ensure the fit is to your liking. If it's still too loose, repeat the process and take in more fabric. If it's too tight, you can let out the seams slightly or start the process over with less fabric marked.

Pressing the Seams

After you have achieved the desired fit, it's important to press the seams to ensure they lay flat and look professional. Use a hot iron and press the seams open, taking care not to create any new creases or wrinkles in the fabric. This step is crucial to achieving a clean and polished look.

Making Small Adjustments

Now that you know how to take in a men's dress shirt, you can make small adjustments to all of your dress shirts to achieve the perfect fit. Don't be afraid to experiment with different stitches or take in different amounts of fabric to find what works best for you.

Conclusion of How to Take in Men's Dress Shirt

Altering a dress shirt may seem daunting at first, but with the right tools and a little bit of practice, it can be a simple and cost-effective solution to ill-fitting clothing. Remember to choose the right stitch, take in equal amounts of fabric on each side, and press the seams for a professional finish. With these tips, you'll be able to achieve the perfect fit on all of your dress shirts.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I alter a dress shirt myself or should I take it to a tailor?
A: It is possible to alter a dress shirt yourself, but if you're not confident in your sewing skills, it's best to take it to a professional tailor.

Q: How much fabric should I take in?
A: It's best to take in equal amounts on each side of the shirt to ensure it remains balanced. Start by taking in a small amount and try the shirt on to see if it's the desired fit.

Q: What kind of fabric pen or chalk should I use?
A: Use a fabric pen or chalk that is easily visible on the fabric but won't leave permanent marks. You can find these at most craft or fabric stores.

Q: Can I let out the seams if I take in too much fabric?
A: Yes, if you take in too much fabric, you can let out the seams slightly or start the process over with less fabric marked.
