Twinkies

Twinkies were a staple in many households in the 90s. These golden sponge cakes filled with creamy vanilla filling were a favorite snack for kids and adults alike. Sadly, Hostess Brands, the company that made Twinkies, went bankrupt in 2012, and the snack disappeared from shelves. Although the snack made a comeback in 2013, it's not the same as the original Twinkies we grew up with.

Squeeze-Its

Squeeze-Its were a popular drink for kids in the 90s. The brightly colored plastic bottles with twist-off tops made them a favorite among children. The drink came in a variety of flavors and was perfect for packing in school lunches. Unfortunately, Squeeze-Its were discontinued in 2001, and we haven't seen them since.

Ouch! Bubble Gum

Ouch! Bubble Gum was a popular gum in the 90s. The gum came in a variety of flavors and was shaped like a band-aid. The packaging was unique, and the gum was fun to chew. Unfortunately, Ouch! Bubble Gum was discontinued in the early 2000s, and we haven't seen it since.

Surge Soda

Surge Soda was a popular soft drink in the 90s. The citrus-flavored soda was marketed towards teenagers and was known for its high caffeine content. Surge was discontinued in 2003 due to poor sales, but fans of the drink started a campaign to bring it back. In 2014, Coca-Cola reintroduced the drink, but it's only available in limited quantities.

Dunkaroos

Dunkaroos were a favorite snack for kids in the 90s. The snack consisted of cookies shaped like kangaroos and a small container of frosting for dipping.
