The History of Hermès

Hermès was founded in Paris in 1837 by Thierry Hermès. At first, the brand focused on creating high-quality harnesses and saddles for horses. However, as the world changed, so did Hermès. The brand expanded into other luxury goods, such as leather bags, silk scarves, and watches.

Hermès has always been a family-run business. Today, the brand is run by the sixth generation of the Hermès family. This has allowed the brand to maintain its core values and traditions while also adapting to the changing world.

The Iconic Hermès Products

Hermès is known for its iconic products, such as the Birkin bag, the Kelly bag, and the silk scarf. These products have become symbols of luxury and are coveted by fashion lovers all over the world.

The Birkin bag is perhaps the most famous Hermès product. This handbag is named after the actress Jane Birkin, who inspired its creation. The Birkin bag is made of the finest leather and is crafted by skilled artisans. It can take up to 18 hours to create a single Birkin bag.

The Kelly bag is another iconic Hermès product. This handbag is named after the actress and princess of Monaco, Grace Kelly. The Kelly bag is known for its classic design and impeccable craftsmanship.

The Hermès silk scarf is also a must-have for any fashion lover. These scarves are made of the finest silk and are printed with intricate designs that are often inspired by nature.

The Hermès Workshop

One of the reasons why Hermès products are so special is because they are made in the brand's own workshops. Hermès has several workshops in France where its products are crafted by skilled artisans.

Each Hermès product is made using traditional techniques that have been passed down through generations of artisans. For example, the Birkin bag is made using a technique called saddle stitching. This technique is used to create a strong and durable seam that can withstand the test of time.
