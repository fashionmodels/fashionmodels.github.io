Name of Traditional Clothes in China: Hanfu

One of the most well-known types of traditional clothing in China is Hanfu. Hanfu is an umbrella term used to describe the clothing worn by the Han Chinese during the Han dynasty. It is a style of clothing that has been passed down through the ages and is still worn by some people in China today.

The name "Hanfu" literally means "clothing of the Han people." It is a style of clothing that has been influenced by various cultures and has evolved over time. Hanfu clothing is characterized by its loose-fitting, flowing design, and intricate embroidery.

Name of Traditional Clothes in China: Qipao

Another popular type of traditional clothing in China is the Qipao. The Qipao, also known as the Cheongsam, is a form-fitting dress that originated in Shanghai in the 1920s. It quickly became a popular style of clothing for women and is still worn by many women in China today.

My personal experience with the Qipao was during a visit to Shanghai. I saw many women dressed in Qipao dresses, and I was struck by how elegant and sophisticated they looked. The Qipao is a symbol of femininity and grace, and it is often worn for special occasions.

The Qipao is characterized by its high collar, form-fitting design, and slit on the side. It is often made from silk or other high-quality fabrics and is adorned with intricate embroidery or beading.
