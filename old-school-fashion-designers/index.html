Discovering The Roots Of Fashion Old School Fashion Designers

Written by Petter Sep 12, 2023 · 5 min read

Do you ever wonder where the fashion industry began? The world of fashion has had a long and winding history, with many designers paving the way for the styles we know and love today. However, not all designers are created equal, and some have truly stood the test of time.

Old school fashion designers have been around for decades, and they've helped shape the fashion industry into what it is today. However, with so many new designers and trends emerging every year, it can be easy to forget about the roots of fashion.

So, who are these old school fashion designers, and why should we care about them? Let's dive in and find out.

The Importance of Old School Fashion Designers

As mentioned earlier, old school fashion designers have laid the foundation for the fashion industry. They were the ones who created the designs that were popularized in their time, and they've inspired many designers that came after them.

One designer that comes to mind when discussing old school fashion is Coco Chanel. She revolutionized women's fashion in the early 20th century, introducing pants and short haircuts for women, and creating the now-iconic Chanel suit. Karl Lagerfeld, who took over Chanel after her death, continued to honor her legacy and cemented her status as one of the greatest fashion designers of all time.

Another notable old school designer is Yves Saint Laurent. He was a pioneer in the industry, introducing the first tuxedo for women, and popularizing the concept of ready-to-wear clothing. His designs were daring and bold, and he was often seen as a rebel in the industry.

A Personal Experience with Old School Fashion Designers

Personally, I have always been drawn to the designs of Christian Dior. His New Look collection in 1947 was a game-changer in the fashion industry, introducing full skirts and nipped-in waists that emphasized an hourglass figure. His designs were elegant and feminine, and he quickly became one of the most influential designers of his time.

What I love about old school fashion designers is that they were true artists. They didn't just create clothing, they created art. Each piece was carefully crafted and designed to perfection, and you could see the passion and dedication in every stitch.

The Legacy of Old School Fashion Designers

The legacy of old school fashion designers can still be seen in the industry today. Many modern designers draw inspiration from their designs, and some even pay homage to them in their collections. For example, Maria Grazia Chiuri, the current creative director of Dior, often references Christian Dior in her designs.

Old school fashion designers have also influenced the way we think about fashion. They showed us that fashion is not just about clothing, but about self-expression and creativity. They taught us that fashion can be a form of art, and that it has the power to make a statement.

Why We Should Celebrate Old School Fashion Designers

It's important to celebrate old school fashion designers because they paved the way for the industry we know and love today. Without them, we wouldn't have the designs and trends that we see on the runway and on the streets.

Old school fashion designers also remind us of the importance of craftsmanship and attention to detail. In a world where fast fashion is king, it's refreshing to look back at a time when clothing was made to last, and every piece was a work of art.

How to Incorporate Old School Fashion into Your Wardrobe

If you're looking to incorporate old school fashion into your wardrobe, there are a few things you can do. First, look for vintage pieces from old school designers like Chanel, Dior, or Saint Laurent. These pieces may be expensive, but they're worth the investment.

You can also look for modern designers who draw inspiration from old school fashion. For example, Gucci's current creative director, Alessandro Michele, often incorporates vintage-inspired designs into his collections. Look for pieces that have a retro vibe, like full skirts, nipped-in waists, and bold prints.

Conclusion of Old School Fashion Designers

Old school fashion designers have left an indelible mark on the fashion industry. They were pioneers in their time, creating designs that were both timeless and daring. Their legacy can still be seen in the industry today, and their designs continue to inspire new generations of designers. So, the next time you're shopping for clothes or watching a fashion show, take a moment to appreciate the work of these old school fashion designers.
