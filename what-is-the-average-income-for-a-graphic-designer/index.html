What Is The Average Income For A Graphic Designer

Written by Robby Jul 28, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you considering a career in graphic design but wondering about the average income? Or maybe you're already a graphic designer looking to see if your salary is competitive. Either way, understanding the average income for a graphic designer is important for making informed career decisions.

The average income for a graphic designer varies depending on factors such as experience, location, and industry. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for graphic designers in the United States was $52,110 in May 2019. However, the top 10% of graphic designers earned more than $89,210, while the lowest 10% earned less than $30,810.

In summary, the average income for a graphic designer is around $52,110, but this can vary based on different factors.

Personal Experience with Graphic Design Income

As a freelance graphic designer with five years of experience, I've found that my income can vary greatly depending on the projects I take on and the clients I work with. Some months I may earn upwards of $5,000, while other months I may only earn $1,000. It can be a bit unpredictable, but overall I feel satisfied with my income as a graphic designer.

Factors Affecting Graphic Design Income

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can affect the income of a graphic designer:

Experience

The more experience a graphic designer has, the more they can typically earn. However, this is not always the case as some designers with less experience may have a strong portfolio and skillset that allows them to earn a high income.

Industry

The industry a graphic designer works in can also affect their income. For example, a designer working in advertising or marketing may earn more than a designer working in publishing.

Continuing Education and Networking

As a graphic designer, it's important to continue learning and growing in your field. Taking courses or attending workshops can help you stay up-to-date on the latest design trends and technologies, which can make you more valuable to clients and potentially increase your income. Networking with other designers and industry professionals can also lead to new job opportunities and higher-paying projects.

Question and Answer

Q: Is a degree necessary to become a graphic designer?

A: While a degree in graphic design can be helpful, it is not always necessary. A strong portfolio and skillset can be just as valuable to potential clients or employers.

Q: Can freelance graphic designers earn more than those working for a company?

A: It's possible for freelance graphic designers to earn more than those working for a company, but it can also be less stable and more unpredictable.

Q: Are there opportunities for graphic designers to earn passive income?

A: Yes, some graphic designers create and sell digital products such as fonts or design templates, which can provide a source of passive income.

Q: How can I negotiate for a higher salary as a graphic designer?

A: Researching industry salaries and highlighting your experience and skills can help you negotiate for a higher salary. It's also important to be confident and assertive in your negotiations.

Conclusion of What is the Average Income for a Graphic Designer

Overall, the average income for a graphic designer is around $52,110, but this can vary significantly based on factors such as experience, industry, and location. By staying up-to-date with the latest design trends and technologies and networking with other designers and industry professionals, you can potentially increase your income as a graphic designer. Remember to always negotiate for fair compensation and be confident in your skills and experience.
