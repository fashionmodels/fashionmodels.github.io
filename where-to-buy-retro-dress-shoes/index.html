Where To Buy Retro Dress Shoes A Guide For Vintage Fashion Enthusiasts

Written by Petter Jul 03, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you a lover of all things vintage, but struggling to find the perfect pair of retro dress shoes? Perhaps you're overwhelmed by the sheer number of options out there, or you're not sure where to start your search. Whatever the case may be, we're here to help.

When it comes to buying retro dress shoes, there are a few pain points that many vintage fashion enthusiasts face. For one, it can be difficult to find shoes that are true to the era you're trying to emulate. Additionally, vintage footwear can be quite expensive and may not always be in the best condition. Finally, many people struggle with finding shoes that fit both their style and their feet.

So, where can you buy retro dress shoes that meet all of your needs? There are actually quite a few options out there, and we'll cover them all below.

First and foremost, it's important to note that you can often find great retro dress shoes at vintage and thrift stores. These stores are a treasure trove of unique, one-of-a-kind pieces, and you might just stumble upon the perfect pair of shoes. That being said, these stores can be hit or miss, so you may need to visit multiple locations and be patient in your search.

If you're looking for a wider selection of retro dress shoes, online marketplaces like Etsy and eBay can be great options. These sites are home to a vast array of vintage clothing and accessories, including shoes. By browsing through the listings and using specific search terms (like "1960s pumps" or "1960s brogues"), you can narrow down your options and find exactly what you're looking for.

You can also find retro dress shoes at specialty vintage retailers, both online and in-person. These stores often have a curated selection of vintage footwear that has been inspected and restored to ensure it's in wearable condition. While these shoes may be pricier than what you'd find at a thrift store, they're generally more affordable than buying brand new shoes with a vintage-inspired design.

Another option is to look for reproduction shoes that are designed to look like vintage styles. These shoes are often made with modern materials and manufacturing techniques, but they have the same aesthetic as retro dress shoes. Some popular brands that make reproduction shoes include B.A.I.T. Footwear, Royal Vintage Shoes, and Miss L Fire.

Personal Experience: Finding Retro Dress Shoes on Etsy

As someone who loves vintage fashion, I've spent a lot of time searching for the perfect pair of retro dress shoes. Recently, I turned to Etsy to try to find a pair of 1950s-style pumps to wear to a wedding. While it took some time to sort through all of the listings and find something that fit my style and budget, I eventually found a beautiful pair of black and white spectator pumps that were just what I was looking for.

One thing to keep in mind when shopping on Etsy (or any online marketplace) is that you should always read the seller's reviews and policies before making a purchase. Look for sellers with a high rating and plenty of positive feedback, and make sure you understand their return and exchange policies in case the shoes don't fit or aren't as described.

Shopping for Reproduction Shoes

If you're not able to find the perfect pair of retro dress shoes in vintage or thrift stores, or you're not comfortable buying shoes online, reproduction shoes can be a great alternative. These shoes are designed to look like vintage styles, but they're made with modern materials and manufacturing techniques, so they're often more comfortable and durable than true vintage shoes.

When shopping for reproduction shoes, it's important to pay attention to the sizing chart and read reviews from other customers to get a sense of how the shoes fit. Some reproduction shoe brands run true to size, while others may run small or large.

Specialty Vintage Retailers

If you're willing to spend a bit more on your retro dress shoes, specialty vintage retailers can be a great option. These stores typically have a curated selection of vintage shoes that have been inspected and restored, so you can be confident that you're getting a high-quality, wearable pair of shoes.

Some popular online vintage retailers that specialize in footwear include The Way We Wore, Beyond Retro, and Rokit. Many of these stores also have physical locations, so if you happen to live near one, it's worth stopping in to see their selection in person.

Caring for Your Retro Dress Shoes

No matter where you buy your retro dress shoes, it's important to take good care of them to ensure they last as long as possible. Depending on the material and condition of the shoes, you may need to use different cleaning and conditioning products.

For leather shoes, it's important to clean them regularly with a leather cleaner and conditioner. You can also use a waterproof spray to protect them from rain and snow. For suede shoes, use a suede brush to remove dirt and stains, and consider using a suede protector spray.

Conclusion of Where to Buy Retro Dress Shoes

Whether you're looking for true vintage shoes or reproduction styles, there are plenty of options out there for retro dress shoe enthusiasts. From vintage and thrift stores to online marketplaces to specialty vintage retailers, the key is to be patient and persistent in your search. And once you do find the perfect pair of shoes, be sure to take good care of them so they can be a part of your wardrobe for years to come.

Question and Answer

Q: Are vintage shoes comfortable to wear?

A: It depends on the shoe and its condition. Some vintage shoes may be more comfortable than others, but it's important to keep in mind that they were likely made with different materials and manufacturing techniques than modern shoes.

Q: Can I return vintage shoes if they don't fit?

A: It depends on the store or seller's return policy. When shopping for vintage shoes, be sure to read the return and exchange policies carefully before making a purchase.

Q: How can I tell if a vintage shoe is in good condition?

A: Look for signs of wear and tear, such as scuffs, scratches, and worn soles. It's also important to check the structural integrity of the shoe, such as the heel and the lining. If you're unsure about the condition of a vintage shoe, ask the seller for more information.

Q: How can I make sure I'm buying authentic vintage shoes?

A: Look for signs of the shoe's age, such as the type of sole and the materials used. Additionally, do some research on the specific era and style of the shoe to make sure it's accurate. If you're still unsure, ask the seller for more information or seek out a vintage expert for advice.
