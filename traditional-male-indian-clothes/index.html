Traditional Male Indian Clothes A Guide To Indian Ethnic Wear

Written by Petter Oct 18, 2023 Â· 5 min read

India is a country rich in culture and tradition, and one of the most fascinating aspects of Indian culture is its traditional clothing. From the vibrant colors to the intricate designs, traditional male Indian clothes are a sight to behold. In this guide, we'll take a look at some of the most popular types of traditional male Indian clothes and what makes them so special.

When it comes to traditional male Indian clothes, one of the biggest pain points for people is figuring out how to wear them. With so many different styles and designs, it can be overwhelming to know where to start. Additionally, some people may be hesitant to wear traditional Indian clothes because they feel like they don't know enough about the culture or may be afraid of cultural appropriation.

The target of traditional male Indian clothes is to celebrate and showcase the rich cultural heritage of India. Wearing traditional Indian clothes is not only a way to connect with the country's past but also to honor its present. It's a way to show appreciation for the artistry and craftsmanship that goes into each piece of clothing.

In this article, we'll explore some of the most popular types of traditional male Indian clothes, including the kurta, dhoti, sherwani, and bandhgala. We'll also provide some tips on how to style each type of clothing and where to wear them.

The Kurta: A Versatile Choice

The kurta is one of the most popular types of traditional male Indian clothes. It is a long, loose shirt that falls just above the knee and is typically made from cotton or silk. The kurta is a versatile choice that can be dressed up or down depending on the occasion.

Personally, I love wearing a kurta paired with jeans for a casual look. It's comfortable and stylish, and the vibrant colors and intricate designs make it stand out. For more formal occasions, a silk kurta paired with a churidar (a type of tight-fitting pants) or a dhoti is a great choice.

The Dhoti: A Timeless Classic

The dhoti is a traditional garment that has been worn in India for centuries. It is a rectangular piece of cloth that is wrapped around the waist and legs, and then tucked into the waistband. The dhoti is a timeless classic that is still worn by many men in India today.

When I was younger, I used to wear a dhoti to weddings and other formal events. It's a bit tricky to wrap correctly, but once you get the hang of it, it's a comfortable and elegant choice. If you're not comfortable wearing a dhoti, you can also opt for a lungi, which is a similar garment but is wrapped differently.

The Sherwani: A Regal Look

The sherwani is a long coat that is typically worn over a kurta and churidar or dhoti. It is a regal and elegant choice that is perfect for weddings and other formal events. The sherwani is often made from silk or other luxurious fabrics and is adorned with intricate embroidery and embellishments.

When I got married, I wore a sherwani for my wedding. It was a deep red color with gold embroidery, and it made me feel like royalty. If you're looking to make a statement at a formal event, a sherwani is definitely the way to go.

The Bandhgala: A More Modern Option

The bandhgala is a type of jacket that is similar to the western suit jacket but has a more traditional Indian look. It is typically worn with a kurta and churidar or dhoti and is a more modern option for those who want to incorporate traditional Indian clothing into their wardrobe.

I love wearing a bandhgala to work events or other formal occasions. It's a great way to stand out from the crowd while still looking professional. I typically pair it with a kurta and churidar and finish the look with some traditional Indian footwear, like jutti or mojari.

Question and Answer Section

Q: Is it appropriate for non-Indians to wear traditional Indian clothes?

A: Yes, it is appropriate for anyone to wear traditional Indian clothes. Just be sure to show respect for the culture and understand the significance of the clothing.

Q: How do I know which type of traditional Indian clothing to wear for a specific occasion?

A: It's always a good idea to check with the host of the event or do some research on the type of event you'll be attending. Generally, more formal events call for more formal clothing, like a sherwani, while casual events may be better suited for a kurta and jeans.

Q: Where can I buy traditional male Indian clothes?

A: There are many online retailers that specialize in traditional Indian clothing, like Utsav Fashion and Cbazaar. You can also find traditional Indian clothing at local Indian stores or boutiques.

Q: How do I care for traditional Indian clothing?

A: Always check the care label on the garment, but generally, traditional Indian clothing should be hand washed or dry cleaned. Avoid using harsh detergents or bleach, and always air dry the clothing.

Conclusion of Traditional Male Indian Clothes

Traditional male Indian clothes are a beautiful way to celebrate the rich cultural heritage of India. From the versatile kurta to the elegant sherwani, there is a type of traditional Indian clothing for every occasion. By understanding the significance of each type of clothing and showing respect for the culture, anyone can incorporate traditional Indian clothing into their wardrobe.
