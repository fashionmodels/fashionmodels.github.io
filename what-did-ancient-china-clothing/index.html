What Did Ancient China Clothing Look Like

Written by Oliver Sep 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you curious about what clothing ancient Chinese people wore? Do you want to learn about the cultural significance and symbolism behind their attire? If so, you've come to the right place. Understanding what did ancient China clothing look like is a fascinating way to explore the history and traditions of this ancient civilization.

Ancient Chinese clothing was not just a way to cover the body; it was a reflection of social status, gender, and occupation. Clothing could also indicate a person's rank in society or the dynasty they belonged to. However, certain clothing items could only be worn by specific groups or classes, leading to a rigid social hierarchy.

Ancient Chinese clothing consisted of several layers, including an inner garment, an outer garment, and a robe. The fabrics used for clothing varied depending on the season and occasion. Silk was the most luxurious and expensive material and was reserved for the upper class. Cotton and hemp were more commonly used for everyday wear.

In summary, ancient Chinese clothing was a reflection of social status, gender, and occupation. The fabrics and styles varied depending on the season and occasion, with silk being the most luxurious material. The clothing also helped to establish a rigid social hierarchy.

What Did Ancient China Clothing Symbolize?

As mentioned earlier, ancient Chinese clothing was not just about covering the body. It was also a way to symbolize a person's social status, gender, and occupation. For example, the color yellow was reserved for the emperor, while red was worn by high-ranking officials and brides. White was the color of mourning and was only worn at funerals.

Additionally, certain patterns and designs on clothing had symbolic meanings. Dragons were a popular motif on clothing, symbolizing power and strength. Clouds represented good luck, while the phoenix symbolized rebirth and renewal.

What Were Some Common Clothing Items in Ancient China?

One of the most iconic clothing items in ancient China was the qipao, also known as the cheongsam. This form-fitting dress became popular during the Qing dynasty and was worn by women of all social classes. Men, on the other hand, typically wore a long gown called a changshan.

Foot binding was also a common practice for women during the Song dynasty. This painful procedure was done to create smaller feet, which were considered more attractive and feminine. Women would wear lotus shoes, which were only a few inches long and forced the foot to arch in an unnatural way.

What Fabrics Were Used in Ancient Chinese Clothing?

Silk was the most luxurious and expensive fabric used in ancient Chinese clothing. It was highly valued for its softness, durability, and sheen. Other fabrics commonly used included cotton, hemp, and ramie. These materials were often dyed in bright colors and decorated with intricate embroidery.

How Did Ancient Chinese Clothing Influence Modern Fashion?

Ancient Chinese clothing has had a significant impact on modern fashion, particularly in the realm of formal wear. The qipao and changshan have inspired modern dresses and suits, while traditional Chinese prints and embroidery have been incorporated into contemporary fashion.

What Can We Learn From Ancient Chinese Clothing?

Ancient Chinese clothing teaches us about the role of clothing in society and the significance of symbolism and tradition. It also reminds us of the importance of preserving cultural heritage and the value of traditional craftsmanship.
