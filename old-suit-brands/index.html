Discovering The Charm Of Old Suit Brands A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Jul 08, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Have you ever wondered about the stories behind old suit brands? The kind of suits that exude timeless charm and elegance, and have graced the wardrobes of stylish gents for generations. If you're someone who appreciates sartorial craftsmanship and wants to learn more about these iconic suit brands, then this guide is for you.

Many people find it daunting to shop for suits, especially when they're looking for something classic and timeless. It can be challenging to sift through the endless options available in the market, and there's always a risk of ending up with an ill-fitting or poorly made suit. This is where old suit brands come in; they offer a sense of reliability and authenticity that modern brands cannot always match.

The target of old suit brands is individuals who prefer classic styles and are looking for high-quality, durable suits that will stand the test of time. These suits are designed to last for decades, and their timeless designs mean that they never go out of style.

In this guide, we'll explore some of the most iconic old suit brands and their unique features, and learn why they remain popular among style enthusiasts today.

Brooks Brothers: The Quintessential American Suit Maker

Brooks Brothers is one of the oldest suit makers in the United States, with a history that dates back to 1818. The brand is known for its classic American style and attention to detail, and has dressed many US Presidents over the years.

I remember my first Brooks Brothers suit like it was yesterday. It was a navy blue two-button suit that fit like a glove. From the moment I put it on, I felt like I could conquer the world. The quality of the fabric and the construction was evident, and I knew that this suit would last me for years to come.

One of the unique features of Brooks Brothers suits is their "sack suit" design, which was popularized in the 1920s. This design features a natural shoulder and a relaxed fit, which gives the wearer a comfortable, casual look. Brooks Brothers also offers a range of customization options, including different lapel styles, pocket types, and button configurations.

Savile Row: The Epitome of English Tailoring

Savile Row is a street in London that is synonymous with bespoke tailoring. The street is home to some of the most renowned tailoring houses in the world, and has been a hub of sartorial craftsmanship for over two centuries.

My first experience with Savile Row was when I visited London for the first time. I had heard so much about the street and was eager to see it for myself. As I walked down the street, I was struck by the sense of history and tradition that permeated the air. The tailoring houses were like museums, with racks of fabric and suits on display.

Savile Row suits are known for their impeccable fit and attention to detail. Each suit is made by hand, with the tailor taking extensive measurements and fittings to ensure that the suit fits the client perfectly. The suits are also made with the finest materials, including wool, cashmere, and silk.

The Importance of Fit in Old Suit Brands

One of the most important things to keep in mind when shopping for old suit brands is the importance of fit. These suits are designed to be worn for years, so it's important that they fit well and are comfortable. Poorly fitting suits can be uncomfortable to wear and can also look unflattering.

The key to a good fit is to make sure that the suit is tailored to your body. This means taking accurate measurements and having the suit fitted by a skilled tailor. It's also important to consider your body shape when choosing a suit. For example, if you have a wider waist, you may want to opt for a suit with a higher waistband to create a more flattering silhouette.

Caring for Your Old Suit Brands

Another important aspect of owning old suit brands is taking care of them. These suits are made to last, but they still require proper care to ensure that they stay in good condition. Here are some tips for caring for your old suit:

  • Hang your suit on a wooden hanger to help maintain its shape
  • Avoid wearing your suit too frequently to prevent wear and tear
  • Have your suit dry cleaned only when necessary to prevent damage to the fabric
  • Store your suit in a cool, dry place to prevent mold and mildew

The Future of Old Suit Brands

As fashion trends come and go, old suit brands remain a constant. They offer a sense of history and tradition that modern brands cannot match. While these suits may not be as popular as they once were, they continue to hold a special place in the hearts of style enthusiasts.

Question and Answer

Q: Are old suit brands expensive?

A: Old suit brands can be expensive, but they are worth the investment. These suits are made to last for years and offer a level of quality and craftsmanship that cannot be matched by modern brands.

Q: How do I know if an old suit brand is right for me?

A: The best way to know if an old suit brand is right for you is to try it on. Visit a tailor or a store that carries the brand and see how it feels. Pay attention to the fit, the quality of the fabric, and the overall style.

Q: Can I have an old suit brand suit customized?

A: Yes, many old suit brands offer customization options. You can choose different lapel styles, pocket types, and button configurations to create a suit that is uniquely yours.

Q: How long can I expect an old suit brand suit to last?

A: With proper care, an old suit brand suit can last for decades. These suits are designed to be worn for years and are made with high-quality materials and construction.

Conclusion of Old Suit Brands

Old suit brands are a testament to the enduring appeal of classic style and sartorial craftsmanship. Whether you prefer the quintessential American style of Brooks Brothers or the impeccable tailoring of Savile Row, these suits offer a level of quality and authenticity that cannot be matched by modern brands. By investing in an old suit brand suit, you're not just buying a piece of clothing, you're buying a piece of history.
