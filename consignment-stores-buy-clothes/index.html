Consignment stores buy clothes from individuals and sell them in their store. The store takes a percentage of the sale, and the rest goes to the original owner of the clothes. This is a win-win situation as it allows people to get rid of their old clothes and make some money at the same time.

How Do Consignment Stores Work?

Consignment stores work by accepting gently used clothing from individuals. The store will then sort through the items and decide which ones to buy. Once the store has purchased the clothing, they will sell it in their store at a marked-up price. The original owner of the clothes will receive a percentage of the sale, and the rest goes to the store.

It's important to note that consignment stores have guidelines for the clothing they will accept. Typically, they will only accept clothing in good condition that is in style and in season. They may also have specific requirements for certain types of clothing, such as designer items.
