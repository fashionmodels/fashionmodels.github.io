Top 10 Men's Ethnic Wear Brands in India: A Guide to the Best Indian Ethnic Wear for Men

When it comes to Indian ethnic wear for men, it can be difficult to know where to start. With so many options available, it can be overwhelming to try and choose the right brand. From traditional styles to modern twists on classic designs, there are a lot of factors to consider. In this guide, we'll take a closer look at the top 10 men's ethnic wear brands in India, and help you find the perfect outfit for any occasion.

If you're looking for high-quality Indian ethnic wear, it's important to choose a brand that offers both style and substance. The top 10 men's ethnic wear brands in India are known for their attention to detail, quality fabrics, and unique designs. Whether you're looking for a classic sherwani, a modern kurta, or a stylish bandhgala, these brands have something for everyone.

So, what are the top 10 men's ethnic wear brands in India? Here's a quick rundown:

1. Manyavar

Manyavar is one of the most popular ethnic wear brands in India, known for its high-quality sherwanis, kurtas, and Indo-western outfits. With a wide range of styles and colors to choose from, Manyavar is a great choice for any occasion.

2. FabIndia

FabIndia offers a unique range of ethnic wear for men, with a focus on traditional handloom fabrics and sustainable fashion. From kurtas to Nehru jackets, FabIndia's collection is both stylish and eco-friendly.

3. Raymond

Raymond is a well-known brand in India, known for its high-quality fabrics and classic designs. From suits to sherwanis, Raymond's collection of ethnic wear is perfect for any formal occasion.

4. Biba

Biba is a popular ethnic wear brand for women, but it also offers a great selection of kurtas and sherwanis for men. With a focus on bright colors and unique prints, Biba is a great choice for a more modern, trendy look.

5. W

W is another women's ethnic wear brand that also offers a selection of men's kurtas and sherwanis. With a focus on traditional styles and vibrant colors, W's collection is perfect for any festive occasion.

6. Manyavar Mohey

Manyavar Mohey is a sub-brand of Manyavar, with a focus on bridal and wedding wear. From intricate sherwanis to stylish kurtas, Manyavar Mohey has everything you need to look your best on your big day.

7. Ritu Kumar

Ritu Kumar is a well-known designer in India, known for her unique blend of traditional and modern designs. Her collection of ethnic wear for men includes everything from kurtas to bandhgalas, all with a modern twist.

8. Tarun Tahiliani

Tarun Tahiliani is another popular designer in India, known for his luxurious fabrics and intricate designs. His collection of ethnic wear for men includes everything from sherwanis to kurta sets, all with a focus on elegance and sophistication.

9. Kunal Rawal

Kunal Rawal is a young designer in India, known for his modern take on traditional Indian menswear. His collection includes a range of contemporary kurtas, jackets, and suits, all with a unique twist.

10. Sabyasachi

Sabyasachi is one of the most famous designers in India, known for his intricate embroidery and stunning designs. His collection of ethnic wear for men includes everything from sherwanis to bandhgalas, all with a focus on luxury and sophistication.

So there you have it, the top 10 men's ethnic wear brands in India. Whether you're looking for a classic sherwani or a modern kurta, these brands offer something for everyone.

How to Choose the Right Ethnic Wear Brand for You

When choosing an ethnic wear brand, it's important to consider your personal style, the occasion, and the level of formality. If you're attending a wedding or a formal event, a brand like Manyavar or Raymond might be a better choice. If you're looking for something more casual or trendy, a brand like Kunal Rawal or Biba might be a better fit. It's also important to consider the quality of the fabrics and the level of craftsmanship, as this will impact the overall look and feel of the outfit.

A: A sherwani is a long, buttoned coat that is worn with a churidar or dhoti, while a bandhgala is a shorter, more structured jacket that is worn with trousers.
