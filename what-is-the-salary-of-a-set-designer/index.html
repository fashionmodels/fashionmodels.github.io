What Is The Salary Of A Set Designer

Written by Christine Jun 04, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Have you ever wondered what a set designer gets paid for their work? If you're interested in pursuing a career in set design or just curious about the industry, then you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll take a closer look at what a set designer's salary is and what factors can influence their earnings.

Set designers often face a number of challenges when it comes to their salary. For one, the entertainment industry is notoriously competitive, which means that there are often more people vying for jobs than there are available positions. Additionally, set designers may find that their earnings can vary greatly depending on things like their level of experience, the type of production they're working on, and the location in which they're based.

What is the Salary of a Set Designer?

The salary of a set designer can vary widely depending on a number of factors. According to data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for set and exhibit designers was $57,290 as of May 2020. However, this figure can fluctuate depending on things like the designer's level of experience, the type of production they're working on, and the location in which they're based.

For example, set designers who work on large-scale productions like feature films or Broadway shows may be able to command significantly higher salaries than those who work on smaller-scale projects like local theater productions. Similarly, designers who are based in major entertainment hubs like New York or Los Angeles may be able to earn more than those who work in smaller cities or towns.

My Personal Experience with Set Design Salaries

As a freelance set designer based in New York City, I've found that my earnings can vary widely depending on the project I'm working on. For example, I've worked on small-scale theater productions where I've earned as little as $500 for a week's worth of work. On the other hand, I've also worked on feature films where I've earned tens of thousands of dollars for just a few weeks of work.

Overall, I'd say that my experience has been that set design salaries can be unpredictable, but that there is potential to earn a good living if you're willing to put in the work and build up your experience and reputation over time.

Factors That Can Influence Set Design Salaries

As we mentioned earlier, there are a number of factors that can influence how much a set designer earns. Some of the most important of these include:

Level of Experience

Like many professions, set design salaries often increase as designers gain more experience and build up their portfolios. Designers who are just starting out may have to accept lower-paying jobs in order to gain experience and build their reputations.

Type of Production

As we mentioned earlier, set designers who work on larger-scale productions like feature films or Broadway shows may be able to command higher salaries than those who work on smaller-scale projects like local theater productions.

Location

Set designers who are based in major entertainment hubs like New York or Los Angeles may be able to earn more than those who work in smaller cities or towns.

How to Negotiate Your Set Design Salary

If you're a set designer who's looking to negotiate your salary, there are a few things you can do to increase your chances of success:

  • Do your research on industry standards for set design salaries in your area.
  • Be prepared to negotiate and make a case for why you believe you deserve a higher salary.
  • Highlight any relevant experience or skills that make you a valuable asset to the production.
  • Consider negotiating other benefits like additional vacation time or a flexible work schedule if the production can't offer you a higher salary.

FAQs About Set Design Salaries

Q: How much do set designers make per hour?

A: The hourly rate for a set designer can vary widely depending on factors like their level of experience, the type of production they're working on, and their location. However, according to data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for set and exhibit designers was $27.54 as of May 2020.

Q: What education do you need to become a set designer?

A: A degree in theater design, scenic design, or a related field can be helpful for aspiring set designers. However, many set designers also gain experience through apprenticeships or on-the-job training.

Q: How much do set designers make on Broadway?

A: Set designers who work on Broadway productions can earn a wide range of salaries depending on the production and their level of experience. However, according to Payscale.com, the average salary for a Broadway set designer is around $70,000 per year.

Q: What other jobs can a set designer do?

A: Set designers may also be able to work in related fields like exhibit design, interior design, or film and television production design.

Conclusion of What is the Salary of a Set Designer

In conclusion, the salary of a set designer can vary widely depending on a number of factors, including the designer's level of experience, the type of production they're working on, and their location. However, with hard work and dedication, it is possible to build a successful career in set design and earn a good living doing what you love.
