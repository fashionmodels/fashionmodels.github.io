What Color Not To Wear To Indian Wedding A Complete Guide

Written by William Nov 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Indian weddings are known for their vibrant colors, elaborate rituals, and grand celebrations. If you are attending an Indian wedding, it is crucial to dress appropriately and avoid any fashion faux pas. One of the most important things to keep in mind is the color of your attire. In this blog post, we will guide you on what color not to wear to Indian weddings.

Understanding the Pain Points

Indian weddings are a celebration of colors, and each color has a significant meaning. Wearing the wrong color can be considered disrespectful or inappropriate. Indian weddings are also a place where you will be meeting new people, and dressing correctly will leave a good impression.

What Color Not to Wear to Indian Wedding and Its Target

The color black is considered inauspicious and is not worn during weddings. It is associated with mourning and sadness. White is also not a suitable color for weddings as it is associated with funerals. Additionally, red is reserved for the bride and should not be worn by guests as it can be seen as a sign of competition. It is also best to avoid wearing bright neon colors or anything too flashy as it can be distracting.

It is essential to dress conservatively and respect the cultural norms of the wedding you are attending. It is also a good idea to ask the wedding hosts or your Indian friends about the dress code and any specific color restrictions.

Personal Experience

As a non-Indian attending my first Indian wedding, I made sure to research the dress code beforehand. I opted for a simple yet elegant green saree, which I later found out was the perfect choice as green symbolizes new beginnings and harmony. I received many compliments on my outfit and felt comfortable and appropriate throughout the wedding festivities.

How to Dress Appropriately for an Indian Wedding

When attending an Indian wedding, it is best to choose traditional attire such as sarees, lehengas, or salwar kameez. Dress modestly and avoid anything too revealing or tight-fitting. Opt for bright colors such as pink, orange, or yellow, but avoid wearing red or black. You can also accessorize with jewelry, bangles, and bindis to complete your look.

Personal Experience

At another Indian wedding, I wore a bright pink lehenga with gold embroidery, accessorized with traditional jewelry. I felt comfortable and confident throughout the wedding ceremonies and received many compliments on my outfit. It is essential to choose an outfit that reflects your personal style while also respecting the cultural norms.

Why Is the Color Black Inauspicious?

Black is associated with death and mourning in many cultures, including Indian culture. It is believed that wearing black during a joyous occasion can bring bad luck and negativity. Moreover, Indian weddings are a time of celebration and happiness, and black is not an appropriate color for such occasions.

How to Choose the Right Colors for an Indian Wedding

When choosing colors for an Indian wedding, it is essential to consider the time of day, the location, and the cultural significance of each color. Bright colors such as pink, orange, and yellow are suitable for daytime events, while darker colors such as navy or maroon are more appropriate for evening events. It is also crucial to avoid wearing the same color as the bride or groom and to choose colors that complement your skin tone.

Personal Experience

At an evening wedding reception, I wore a navy blue saree with golden embroidery, paired with traditional Indian jewelry. The color was appropriate for the time of day and the event, and I felt comfortable and stylish throughout the night.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I wear white if it has gold embroidery?

A: It is best to avoid wearing white altogether, even if it has gold embroidery, as white is associated with funerals in Indian culture.

Q: Can I wear a black saree with bright jewelry?

A: It is not advisable to wear black to an Indian wedding, even with bright jewelry. Instead, opt for brighter colors that are appropriate for the occasion.

Q: Is it okay to wear a red saree to an Indian wedding?

A: No, it is not appropriate to wear a red saree to an Indian wedding as it is reserved for the bride.

Q: Can I wear a neon-colored dress to an Indian wedding?

A: It is best to avoid wearing neon colors or anything too flashy as it can be distracting and not appropriate for the occasion.

Conclusion of What Color Not to Wear to Indian Wedding

Attending an Indian wedding can be an exciting and unique experience, but it is essential to dress appropriately and respectfully. Avoid wearing black or white and leave the color red for the bride. Opt for bright colors and traditional attire, and accessorize with jewelry and bindis to complete your look. By following these simple guidelines, you can ensure that you dress appropriately and leave a good impression on the wedding hosts and guests.
