Discover The Best Vintage Clothes Markets In London

Written by Oliver Jul 03, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you a vintage lover looking for the best places to shop in London? Do you want to score some unique and affordable pieces that will enhance your wardrobe? If so, you're in the right place. In this post, we'll guide you through the best vintage clothes markets in London and help you find your next favorite vintage piece.

If you're looking for vintage clothes, you may know that it can be a challenge to find the right pieces. Not all shops have a varied selection or affordable prices, and it can be overwhelming to navigate London's many vintage stores. However, shopping at vintage clothes markets can be a great way to find unique pieces at reasonable prices, all in one place.

The target of this post is to provide a guide to the best vintage clothes markets in London. We'll cover the most popular markets and give you tips on how to make the most of your vintage shopping experience.

In summary, this post will cover the best vintage clothes markets in London, including Brick Lane Market, Camden Market, Portobello Road Market, and Greenwich Market. We'll also share tips on how to navigate these markets and find the best pieces.

Brick Lane Market

Brick Lane Market is a must-visit destination for vintage lovers. This market takes place every Sunday and is known for its eclectic mix of vintage and retro clothing, as well as furniture, homeware, and food stalls. With over 200 stalls, you're bound to find something unique that catches your eye.

Personally, I love visiting Brick Lane Market for vintage clothes shopping. I always find something interesting, whether it's a vintage band t-shirt or a 70s maxi dress. The prices are also reasonable, and you can often negotiate with the vendors to get a better deal.

Camden Market

Camden Market is another popular destination for vintage clothes shopping. This market is open every day and features a wide range of vintage and alternative fashion stalls. You'll find everything from vintage denim to 80s punk clothing to retro sportswear.

When I visited Camden Market, I was impressed by the variety of vintage clothing available. There are stalls dedicated to different eras and styles, so you can easily find what you're looking for. However, the prices can be higher than other vintage markets, so be prepared to haggle.

Portobello Road Market

Portobello Road Market is a charming and picturesque market in Notting Hill. This market is known for its antiques and vintage stalls, but you'll also find plenty of vintage clothing on offer. The market takes place every Saturday, and it's worth arriving early to beat the crowds.

When I visited Portobello Road Market, I was struck by the variety of vintage clothing available. There were stalls selling everything from vintage Chanel to 60s mod dresses. However, the prices can be higher than other markets, so it's worth shopping around before making a purchase.

Greenwich Market

Greenwich Market is a hidden gem for vintage clothes shopping. This market takes place every day and features a range of vintage and retro clothing stalls. You'll also find vintage jewelry, accessories, and homeware on offer.

When I visited Greenwich Market, I was impressed by the quality of the vintage clothing available. The prices were also reasonable, and I was able to find some unique pieces that I hadn't seen elsewhere. Plus, the market has a great atmosphere and plenty of food stalls to keep you fueled for your vintage shopping adventure.

Tips for Vintage Clothes Shopping in London

Now that you know the best vintage clothes markets in London, here are some tips to help you make the most of your vintage shopping experience:

1. Arrive early: Many vintage markets can get crowded, so it's worth arriving early to beat the crowds and get first pick of the best pieces.

2. Bring cash: While some vendors may accept card payments, it's always a good idea to bring cash for your vintage shopping adventure. Plus, it can be easier to negotiate prices when you're paying with cash.

3. Wear comfortable shoes: Vintage shopping can be a workout, so make sure you wear comfortable shoes that can handle a lot of walking and standing.

4. Shop around: While it's tempting to buy the first vintage piece that catches your eye, it's worth shopping around to compare prices and quality. You may find a better deal or a higher quality piece elsewhere.

How to Care for Vintage Clothes

If you're investing in vintage clothes, it's important to take care of them properly to ensure they last. Here are some tips for caring for your vintage pieces:

1. Read the care labels: Vintage clothes may have different care instructions than modern clothes, so make sure you read the care labels carefully before washing or ironing.

2. Hand wash or dry clean: Vintage clothes may be delicate or made from special materials, so it's best to hand wash or dry clean them to avoid damage.

3. Store carefully: Vintage clothes should be stored carefully to avoid damage from sunlight, moisture, or pests. Use acid-free tissue paper to wrap delicate items, and store them in a cool and dry place.

Conclusion of Best Vintage Clothes Markets in London

In conclusion, London is a vintage lover's paradise, with plenty of markets offering unique and affordable vintage pieces. Whether you're looking for 80s punk clothing or vintage Chanel, there's a market for you. By following our tips for vintage shopping and caring for your vintage pieces properly, you can build a wardrobe that's both stylish and sustainable.

Question and Answer

Q: Are vintage clothes markets in London expensive?

A: The prices at vintage clothes markets in London can vary depending on the vendor and the quality of the piece. However, in general, vintage clothes markets offer more affordable prices than vintage boutiques or high-end stores.

Q: Can you negotiate prices at vintage clothes markets in London?

A: Yes, many vendors at vintage clothes markets in London are open to negotiation. It's always worth asking if they can offer a lower price, especially if you're buying multiple items.

Q: Are there any vintage clothes markets in London that are open during the week?

A: Yes, some vintage clothes markets in London, such as Greenwich Market, are open every day. However, it's worth checking the market's website or social media pages for their opening hours.

Q: Can you find designer vintage pieces at vintage clothes markets in London?

A: Yes, some vintage clothes markets in London, such as Portobello Road Market, have vendors who specialize in designer vintage pieces. However, these pieces may be more expensive than other vintage items at the market.
