Beethoven's Moonlight Sonata

One of the most recognizable classical pieces in movies is Beethoven's Moonlight Sonata. This hauntingly beautiful piano composition has been featured in countless films, including The King's Speech, Immortal Beloved, and Twilight. For me personally, the first time I heard Moonlight Sonata in a movie was in The Immigrant, a period drama set in 1920s New York. The piece perfectly captured the mood of the scene, which was both melancholy and reflective. What makes Moonlight Sonata so memorable is its simple yet powerful melody, which can evoke a range of emotions in listeners.

Mozart's Requiem

Another famous classical piece in movies is Mozart's Requiem, which has been used in films such as Amadeus, The Big Lebowski, and X-Men: Apocalypse. The Requiem is a mass for the dead, and its dramatic and powerful choral arrangements make it ideal for intense or somber scenes. I first heard the Requiem in Amadeus, a biographical drama about the life of Mozart. The use of the piece in the film's climax, when Mozart's body is being taken to a pauper's grave, was particularly poignant and emotional. Mozart's Requiem is a timeless masterpiece that continues to inspire and move audiences today.

By understanding the context and history of classical pieces, we can appreciate their use in movies even more.
