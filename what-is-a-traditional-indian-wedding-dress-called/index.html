What Is A Traditional Indian Wedding Dress Called

Written by Luffy Oct 28, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Indian weddings are known for their colorful and vibrant celebrations that last for days. One of the most important aspects of an Indian wedding is the attire worn by the bride and groom. The traditional Indian wedding dress is a unique and beautiful garment that has been worn for centuries. In this blog post, we will explore what a traditional Indian wedding dress is called and provide an in-depth look at its history and significance.

Pain Points Related to Traditional Indian Wedding Dress

Choosing the right wedding dress can be a daunting task for any bride, but it can be especially challenging for those who are not familiar with traditional Indian wedding attire. With so many different styles, colors, and fabrics to choose from, it can be overwhelming to know where to begin.

What is a Traditional Indian Wedding Dress Called?

The traditional Indian wedding dress is called a "lehenga" or "lengha choli." It consists of a long skirt, a top or blouse, and a scarf or dupatta. The skirt is usually heavily embroidered and may be made of silk, satin, or other luxurious fabrics. The top or blouse is also adorned with intricate embroidery and may be sleeveless or have short or long sleeves. The dupatta is a long scarf that is draped over the head and shoulders.

The lehenga choli is typically worn by the bride on her wedding day, but it may also be worn by other women in the bridal party or guests at the wedding. The colors and designs of the lehenga choli vary depending on the region of India and the bride's personal style.

The Significance of the Traditional Indian Wedding Dress

The traditional Indian wedding dress has a deep cultural and historical significance. It symbolizes the bride's heritage, family traditions, and status. The intricate embroidery and luxurious fabrics used in the lehenga choli represent wealth and prosperity. The colors of the dress are also significant, with red being the most popular color as it represents love, passion, and fertility.

For many brides, wearing a traditional Indian wedding dress is not just about looking beautiful, but it is also about honoring their cultural heritage and family traditions.

The Evolution of the Traditional Indian Wedding Dress

Over the years, the traditional Indian wedding dress has evolved to incorporate modern fashion trends. While the basic design of the lehenga choli remains the same, designers have experimented with different fabrics, styles, and colors to create unique and modern interpretations of the traditional dress. Some brides may opt for a more contemporary look by choosing a lehenga choli with a western-style blouse or by incorporating trendy accessories into their outfit.

How to Choose the Perfect Traditional Indian Wedding Dress

Choosing the perfect traditional Indian wedding dress can be a daunting task, but with some careful consideration, it can also be a rewarding and enjoyable experience. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Research different styles and designs before making a decision.
  • Consider the season and location of your wedding when choosing fabrics and colors.
  • Try on different styles to find the one that suits your body type and personal style.
  • Don't be afraid to incorporate modern elements into your outfit.

Personal Experience with Traditional Indian Wedding Dress

As an Indian woman myself, I had the opportunity to wear a traditional lehenga choli for my wedding day. Choosing the perfect dress was a long process, but it was also one of the most enjoyable parts of planning my wedding. I ultimately chose a bright red lehenga choli with gold embroidery, which was a nod to my cultural heritage and personal style. Wearing the dress made me feel like a queen and I will always cherish the memories of that day.

Question and Answer

Q: Is the traditional Indian wedding dress only worn by brides?

A: While the traditional Indian wedding dress is typically worn by the bride, it is not exclusive to her. Other women in the bridal party or guests at the wedding may also wear a lehenga choli.

Q: Can a traditional Indian wedding dress be customized to fit my personal style?

A: Yes, many designers offer customization options for traditional Indian wedding dresses, allowing you to incorporate your personal style and preferences into the design.

Q: What accessories are typically worn with a traditional Indian wedding dress?

A: Traditional Indian jewelry, such as gold bangles, earrings, and necklaces, is often worn with a lehenga choli. The dupatta may also be adorned with jewelry, such as brooches or pins.

Q: How do I choose the right color for my traditional Indian wedding dress?

A: The color of your dress may depend on personal preference, cultural traditions, or the season and location of your wedding. Red is the most popular color for traditional Indian wedding dresses, but other popular colors include gold, pink, and green.

Conclusion of What is a Traditional Indian Wedding Dress Called

The traditional Indian wedding dress, known as a lehenga choli, is a beautiful and significant garment that has been worn for centuries. It represents a bride's cultural heritage, family traditions, and status, and is an important part of any Indian wedding celebration. Whether you choose a classic or modern interpretation of the dress, wearing a lehenga choli is a meaningful and unforgettable experience.
