The Fight of the Century: Muhammad Ali vs. Joe Frazier

The first most popular boxing match we will explore is the Fight of the Century, which took place on March 8, 1971. It featured two of the greatest fighters of all time, Muhammad Ali and Joe Frazier. This was a highly anticipated fight, as both fighters were undefeated at the time.

Ali had been stripped of his title after refusing to be drafted into the Vietnam War, and had been out of the ring for three years. Frazier had won the vacant title, and many people thought he would be too much for Ali to handle. The fight was held at Madison Square Garden in New York City, and was one of the most-watched sporting events in history.

The fight was a brutal affair, with both fighters trading blows and neither giving an inch. In the end, it was Frazier who emerged victorious, winning by unanimous decision. The fight was a turning point in Ali's career, as he would go on to lose two more fights in the coming years before making a comeback and regaining his title.

The Thrilla in Manila: Muhammad Ali vs. Joe Frazier III

The second most popular boxing match we will explore is the Thrilla in Manila, which took place on October 1, 1975. It was the third and final fight between Muhammad Ali and Joe Frazier, and was held in the Philippines.

Both fighters were past their prime, but this did not detract from the excitement surrounding the fight. The two men had a fierce rivalry, and the previous two fights had been closely contested. The Thrilla in Manila would prove to be no different.

The fight was a grueling affair, with both fighters pushing each other to their limits. In the end, it was Ali who emerged victorious, as Frazier's corner threw in the towel after the 14th round. The fight is considered by many to be one of the greatest in boxing history, and it cemented Ali's legacy as one of the greatest fighters of all time.

The Rumble in the Jungle: Muhammad Ali vs. George Foreman

The third most popular boxing match we will explore is the Rumble in the Jungle, which took place on October 30, 1974. It featured Muhammad Ali and George Foreman, two of the most dominant fighters of their era.

The fight was held in Kinshasa, Zaire (now the Democratic Republic of the Congo), and was one of the most-watched sporting events of all time. Ali was considered the underdog, as Foreman had a reputation as a fierce and relentless fighter.
