Top Ten Readymade Garment Brands In India A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline May 21, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of sifting through endless options for quality readymade garments in India? Do you feel overwhelmed by the sheer number of brands claiming to offer the best clothes? In this post, we will explore the top ten readymade garment brands in India and help you make an informed decision.

When it comes to readymade garments, the Indian market offers an abundance of choices. However, with so many brands vying for your attention, it can be hard to determine which ones are worth your time and money.

In this post, we will provide a comprehensive guide to the top ten readymade garment brands in India. We will cover each brand's target audience, style, price range, and unique selling points to help you make an informed decision.

1. Allen Solly

As someone who has always been particular about the clothes I wear, I can vouch for Allen Solly's quality and style. This brand offers a wide range of readymade garments for both men and women, including shirts, trousers, dresses, and accessories.

Allen Solly's target audience is young professionals who want to look stylish and sophisticated without breaking the bank. The brand's signature style is a fusion of traditional and modern, making it a hit with millennials and Gen Z.

Price-wise, Allen Solly is mid-range, with prices starting at around Rs. 1000 for a shirt and going up to Rs. 5000 for a dress. However, the quality of the clothes justifies the price, and you can be sure that your purchase will last a long time.

2. W

W is a women's wear brand that offers a wide range of readymade garments, including kurtis, dresses, and sarees. What sets W apart from other brands is its commitment to sustainability and ethical fashion.

The brand's target audience is women who want to look stylish while also making a positive impact on the environment. W's clothes are made from eco-friendly materials, and the brand has implemented several sustainable practices to reduce its carbon footprint.

Price-wise, W is mid-range, with prices starting at around Rs. 1000 for a kurti and going up to Rs. 5000 for a saree. While the prices may be slightly higher than other brands, you can be sure that your purchase is sustainable and ethical.
