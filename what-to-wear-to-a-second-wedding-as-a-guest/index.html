What To Wear To A Second Wedding As A Guest A Guide

Written by Robby Jun 29, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Attending a wedding is always exciting, but figuring out what to wear can be stressful, especially if it's a second wedding. You want to look your best without upstaging the bride and groom, while also keeping in mind the wedding's dress code and location. In this guide, we'll walk you through what to wear to a second wedding as a guest, so you can feel confident and comfortable on the big day.

When it comes to dressing for a second wedding, there are a few things to keep in mind. First and foremost, you want to avoid wearing white or anything too flashy that might take attention away from the bride and groom. You should also consider the dress code and location of the wedding, as well as the time of year and time of day. It's always better to be slightly overdressed than underdressed, so opt for dressy attire over casual wear.

If you're not sure what the dress code is, check the invitation or ask the bride or groom. If it's a formal wedding, a long dress or dressy suit is appropriate for women, while men should wear a suit and tie. For semi-formal weddings, a cocktail dress or dressy separates are a good choice for women, while men can wear a suit without a tie. For casual weddings, you can opt for a sundress or skirt and blouse for women, and slacks and a button-down shirt for men.

In summary, when deciding what to wear to a second wedding as a guest, keep in mind the dress code, location, time of year, and time of day. Avoid wearing white or anything too flashy, and don't be afraid to dress up rather than down. Now let's dive deeper into some specific outfit ideas for different types of weddings.

Outdoor Wedding

For an outdoor wedding, you want to dress appropriately for the weather while still looking stylish. A flowy sundress or maxi dress is a great option for women, paired with comfortable sandals or wedges. Men can opt for slacks or khakis with a button-down shirt or polo, paired with loafers or dress shoes. If the wedding is taking place on grass, avoid wearing stilettos or heels that might sink into the ground.

Beach Wedding

A beach wedding calls for light, airy fabrics and comfortable footwear. Women can wear a sundress or maxi dress in a bright, tropical color, paired with sandals or espadrilles. Men can opt for a linen suit or slacks with a lightweight button-down shirt or polo, paired with loafers or boat shoes. Avoid wearing anything too formal or heavy, as the beach setting is more relaxed.

City Wedding

For a city wedding, you want to dress up a bit more than you would for a casual or outdoor wedding. Women can wear a cocktail dress or dressy separates, paired with heels or dressy flats. Men can opt for a suit and tie or a dressy blazer with slacks and a button-down shirt, paired with dress shoes. Avoid wearing anything too flashy or revealing, as city weddings tend to be more formal.

Country Wedding

For a country wedding, you want to dress in a way that's both stylish and practical. Women can wear a flowy sundress or maxi dress in a floral or pastel print, paired with boots or comfortable sandals. Men can opt for slacks or khakis with a button-down shirt or polo, paired with boots or loafers. If the wedding is taking place on a farm or in a barn, avoid wearing anything too delicate or fancy that might get ruined.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I wear black to a second wedding?

A: Yes, black is a perfectly acceptable color to wear to a second wedding, especially if it's an evening wedding or a formal wedding. Just make sure to accessorize with colorful or metallic jewelry to add some visual interest.

Q: Can I wear a jumpsuit to a second wedding?

A: Yes, jumpsuits are a stylish and comfortable option for a second wedding, especially if it's a semi-formal or casual wedding. Just make sure to choose a jumpsuit in a dressy fabric like silk or satin, and accessorize with heels and statement jewelry.

Q: Can I wear white to a second wedding if it's not a traditional wedding?

A: It's still best to avoid wearing white to a second wedding, even if it's not a traditional wedding. You don't want to accidentally upstage the bride or draw attention away from her on her special day.

Q: Can I wear jeans to a second wedding?

A: It's best to avoid wearing jeans to a second wedding, even if it's a casual wedding. Opt for dressy separates like a skirt and blouse or slacks and a button-down shirt instead.

Conclusion of What to Wear to a Second Wedding as a Guest

Attending a second wedding as a guest can be a bit tricky when it comes to figuring out what to wear, but with these tips and outfit ideas, you should be able to find the perfect outfit that's both stylish and appropriate for the occasion. Remember to consider the dress code, location, and time of day when choosing your outfit, and avoid wearing white or anything too flashy. With these guidelines in mind, you'll be sure to look and feel your best on the big day.
