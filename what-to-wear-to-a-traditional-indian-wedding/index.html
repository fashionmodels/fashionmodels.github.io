What To Wear To A Traditional Indian Wedding A Complete Guide

Written by Frank Oct 10, 2023 · 5 min read

Attending a traditional Indian wedding can be a thrilling experience, but it can also be stressful when it comes to choosing what to wear. With so many cultural nuances and customs to consider, it's hard to know where to start. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about what to wear to a traditional Indian wedding, from the dress code to the cultural significance of certain outfits.

When it comes to attending a traditional Indian wedding, the dress code is typically formal and conservative. Women are expected to wear traditional Indian attire, while men should wear a formal suit or traditional Indian clothing. It's important to keep in mind that the wedding ceremony may take place in a temple, so dress modestly and avoid any revealing clothing.

First and foremost, women should consider wearing a saree, which is a long piece of fabric that is draped around the body. Sarees come in a variety of styles and fabrics, and they are a great way to showcase traditional Indian fashion. Alternatively, women can wear a salwar kameez, which is a two-piece outfit consisting of a long tunic top and loose pants.

For men, the most common traditional attire is the sherwani, which is a long coat-like garment that is typically worn over a kurta (a long shirt) and churidar (tight-fitting pants). Alternatively, men can wear a formal suit, but it's important to keep in mind that traditional Indian clothing is more appropriate.

In summary, when attending a traditional Indian wedding, it's important to dress conservatively and wear traditional Indian attire if possible. Women can opt for a saree or salwar kameez while men can wear a sherwani or traditional Indian clothing. Remember to avoid any revealing clothing and dress modestly.

My Personal Experience with What to Wear to a Traditional Indian Wedding

As someone who has attended multiple traditional Indian weddings, I can attest to the importance of dressing appropriately. At my first Indian wedding, I made the mistake of wearing a dress that was too revealing. I quickly realized my mistake and felt uncomfortable for the entirety of the wedding ceremony. Since then, I've made sure to dress conservatively and wear traditional Indian attire when possible. Not only does it show respect for the culture, but it also allows me to fully immerse myself in the wedding experience.

The Cultural Significance of Traditional Indian Attire

Traditional Indian attire has a rich cultural significance that goes beyond just fashion. Each outfit has its own history and meaning, from the intricate embroidery to the vibrant colors. For example, red is a popular color for weddings as it symbolizes love and purity. By wearing traditional Indian attire to a wedding, you are not only showing respect for the culture but also honoring the history and tradition behind each outfit.

Understanding the Different Types of Sarees and Salwar Kameez

As mentioned earlier, sarees and salwar kameez are the most common traditional Indian attire for women. However, it's important to note that there are many different types of sarees and salwar kameez to choose from. Some popular types of sarees include the Banarasi saree, which is made of silk and features intricate embroidery, and the Kanjeevaram saree, which is known for its vibrant colors and gold thread work. Salwar kameez also come in a variety of styles, from the simple cotton salwar kameez to the more elaborate Anarkali suit.

Accessorizing Your Traditional Indian Attire

When it comes to accessorizing your traditional Indian attire, less is often more. It's best to stick to simple jewelry that complements your outfit rather than detracts from it. Some popular accessories include bangles, earrings, and necklaces. For men, a simple watch or cufflinks can add a touch of elegance to their traditional Indian outfit.

How to Dress for a Traditional Indian Wedding on a Budget

Traditional Indian attire can be expensive, but there are ways to dress for a wedding on a budget. Consider renting an outfit rather than buying one, or look for sales and discounts at local Indian clothing stores. You can also opt for a simpler outfit rather than a more elaborate one. Remember, it's not about how expensive your outfit is but rather how respectfully you are representing the culture.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I wear Western clothing to a traditional Indian wedding?

A: While Western clothing is not typically worn to a traditional Indian wedding, it is possible to wear a formal suit if you do not have access to traditional Indian attire. However, it's important to keep in mind that traditional Indian clothing is more appropriate.

Q: What colors should I avoid wearing to a traditional Indian wedding?

A: It's best to avoid wearing white or black to a traditional Indian wedding, as these colors are traditionally associated with mourning. Red is a popular color for weddings as it symbolizes love and purity.

Q: Do I need to wear a head covering to a traditional Indian wedding?

A: It's not necessary to wear a head covering to a traditional Indian wedding, but it's important to dress modestly and avoid any revealing clothing.

Q: Can I wear jeans to a traditional Indian wedding?

A: Jeans are not appropriate for a traditional Indian wedding. It's important to dress formally and wear traditional Indian attire if possible.

Conclusion of What to Wear to a Traditional Indian Wedding

Attending a traditional Indian wedding can be a wonderful experience, but it's important to dress appropriately and respectfully. Women should consider wearing a saree or salwar kameez while men can opt for a sherwani or traditional Indian clothing. Remember to dress conservatively and avoid any revealing clothing. By following these guidelines, you can fully immerse yourself in the wedding experience and show respect for the culture.
